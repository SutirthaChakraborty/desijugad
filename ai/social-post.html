How to use the AI Social Post Maker

Get a free Replicate API key

Sign up at replicate.com. Go to Account → API tokens and click Create token. New accounts get $5 free credit — enough for ~50 posts. Copy the token (starts with r8_).

Save your key in AI Settings

Click Add Replicate key above, or go to AI Studio → Settings. Paste your token and click Save. Your key is stored only in your browser — Desi Jugad never sees it.

Upload your logo and fill in brand details

Upload your company logo (PNG or JPG recommended). Add an optional reference image for mood/style. Write a short description of what the post should say — be specific about offer, audience, and visual style for best results.

Select platforms and click Generate Post

Pick the platforms you want to post on. The image is generated once and then cropped to each format. Generation takes 15–45 seconds depending on Replicate load.

Generate captions (optional — needs Gemini key)

Add a free Gemini API key to also generate platform-ready captions and hashtags. The AI looks at your generated image and writes 3 caption variants. Save them as a .txt file to edit in Notion or Google Docs.

Download and share

Download the platform-cropped image for each size you need. For Facebook, LinkedIn, and Twitter/X you can use the Share buttons to open a pre-filled post. For Instagram, download the image first, then tap the Instagram share button to open the app.

💡 Tip — getting the best results:
  • Use a white or transparent background logo for cleanest integration.
  • Be specific in your description — "Diwali sale, 30% off, warm gold tones, elegant Indian aesthetic" beats "post for sale".
  • Try Festive tone for seasonal campaigns, Luxury for premium brands.
  • Add a reference image of a post you admire to guide the visual style.
  • If the first result isn't right, click Generate Post again — each run uses a different seed.

AI-powered social media posts — branded, consistent, fast

Creating good social media visuals usually means hiring a graphic designer, buying Canva Pro, or spending hours tweaking templates. Desi Jugad's Social Post Maker uses Google's Nano Banana model on Replicate to generate branded post images from your logo in under a minute — at less than ₹10 per image.

What is Google Nano Banana?

Nano Banana is an image generation model from Google optimised for product and brand visuals. It accepts multiple reference images and a text prompt, and generates a new image that incorporates elements from all provided references. By feeding it your logo and a brand description, it creates original social post images that are visually consistent with your brand identity.

Platform size guide

Facebook / LinkedIn: 1200×630px (landscape, ~1.91:1 ratio) works best for link previews and standard posts. Twitter/X: 1200×675px (16:9) is optimised for in-feed display. Instagram square: 1080×1080px is the classic Instagram format, works in feeds and grid. Instagram portrait: 1080×1350px (4:5) takes up more screen space in the feed — higher engagement. Stories: 1080×1920px (9:16) for Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp status.

Caption generation with Gemini

After generating your image, you can use a free Google Gemini API key to auto-generate platform-ready captions. The AI analyses your generated image and writes 3 caption variants (short, medium, and long-form) plus 10–15 relevant hashtags. Edit them directly in the browser and download as .txt.

Frequently asked questions

My API key doesn't work — what do I do?

Make sure you copied the full token from Replicate — it starts with r8_ and is quite long. Go to AI Settings, delete the old key, and paste again. If the error says "Out of credits", top up your Replicate account at replicate.com/account/billing. New accounts get $5 free credit automatically.

How long does image generation take?

Usually 15–45 seconds. Replicate sometimes queues requests during peak hours, which can add 30–60 seconds. The progress bar will show current status. If it times out after 2 minutes, click Generate again.

Can I use this for multiple brands?

Yes — just upload different logos each time. Your API key stays saved; everything else resets between sessions. For multiple brands in one session, click New post to start fresh.

The generated image doesn't match my brand style. How do I improve it?

Try being more specific in your brand description. Mention specific colours ("navy blue and gold"), aesthetic ("clean minimal"), and content ("show a product flat lay"). Add a reference image of a post you like. Try a different tone chip. Each generation uses a different seed, so running it again will produce a different result.

Can I share directly to Instagram?

Instagram doesn't allow direct image posting from the web. The Instagram share button downloads your image and opens Instagram's website where you can continue the post on mobile. For desktop publishing, use Meta Business Suite.

Does this work for Indian regional brand names?

Yes. The model handles Indian brand names and Hinglish descriptions well. For scripts like Devanagari or Tamil in the prompt, results may vary — using transliterated brand names in the prompt tends to work better than native script for image generation models.
