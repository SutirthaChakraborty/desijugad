Generate full AI songs with vocals, lyrics, and cover art

AI music generation has reached a point where the output is genuinely listenable. Suno V5.5 (the latest model as of April 2026) can generate full songs with coherent lyrics, melody, and production quality that could pass for independent music on first listen. The key question is pricing — most tools wrap Suno with a subscription and margin. We don't.

Desi Jugad Music Studio connects to your own Suno API wrapper key. You pay the wrapper provider at their published rate (typically $0.01–$0.05 per generation, depending on the wrapper). Lyria 3 (for instrumentals) goes through Replicate at their standard rate.

Suno vs Lyria — which to use?

Suno V5.5 is the choice when you want vocals and lyrics — it generates full songs with singing, production, and cover art. It handles diverse genres well and is particularly strong on pop, folk, hip-hop, and electronic. Lyria 3 Pro (from Google, via Replicate) is for instrumental composition — cinematic scores, background music, jazz, classical. It doesn't generate lyrics or vocals but produces more musically structured output. MusicGen (Meta, open source) is a cheaper option for background loops and ambient music.

Getting a longer song

Suno generates songs in clips. If you want a 3–4 minute track, use the 4-minute length option and our system chains multiple Suno generations automatically: first clip → extend → extend until target duration, then presents the combined result. This takes more time (2–5 minutes) and costs proportionally more, but you get a complete song with a proper structure.

Writing good style prompts

The style field works like a tag list. Be specific: "lofi, acoustic guitar, female vocals, 80bpm, rain ambience, introspective" produces much better results than just "lofi". Include mood words (melancholic, euphoric, tense), instruments (sitar, tabla, synth bass), and tempo descriptors (upbeat, slow, mid-tempo). Negative tags help too — if you don't want distorted guitars, add "heavy metal, distortion" to the negative tags.

Frequently asked questions

Why does Suno return two variations?

Suno generates two versions of every song by default. This gives you options — sometimes one variation nails the mood, sometimes the other. Both are shown as separate cards; pick your favourite and discard (or download) both.

Can I download the MP3?

Yes. Each variation card has a "Download MP3" button that fetches the audio URL from Suno and downloads it directly. You can also send it straight to the Music Video Maker.

What is "Extend"?

After generating a 30-second clip, the Extend button takes the last few seconds of that clip as a seed and generates a continuation. Repeat to build up longer tracks.

Does the generated music have copyright restrictions?

Suno's terms of service apply. Commercial use rights vary by Suno plan (some plans only allow non-commercial use). Check your Suno wrapper provider's terms — sunoapi.org passes through Suno's terms.

Can I generate music in Hindi or other Indian languages?

Yes. Suno V5.5 supports Hindi, Tamil, Bengali, and other Indian languages. Write your lyrics in the target language in the Custom tab, or describe the language in your style prompt ("Hindi lyrics, Punjabi bhangra fusion").
