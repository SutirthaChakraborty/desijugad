How the AI Music Video Maker works

Making a music video used to require a film crew, editing software, and a significant budget. Desi Jugad's AI Music Video Maker changes that. You upload your track, describe what you want the video to look like, and the tool handles everything else — from generating individual video clips to stitching them together with your original audio perfectly synced.

Step-by-step process

When you click Generate, here's what actually happens: First, your audio file is uploaded to a temporary hosting service (tmpfiles.org by default, with file.io as fallback) to get a public URL that Replicate can fetch. This is necessary because Replicate's servers need to access the audio file for analysis and processing. The file expires after 1 hour and is not stored by Desi Jugad.

Next, depending on your chosen duration strategy, the tool calculates how many video clips are needed. For "Cover every second" mode, it divides your song duration by the model's clip length (typically 8 seconds) and generates that many clips sequentially. For "Rhythm cuts" mode, it runs a beat detection pass on your audio using the Web Audio API's AnalyserNode, finds the most energetic moments, and generates clips only at those points.

Once all clips are downloaded, ffmpeg.wasm (a WebAssembly port of ffmpeg that runs entirely in your browser) concatenates them, overlays your original audio track, and applies crossfades at beat boundaries. The final MP4 is assembled locally — it never leaves your device.

Choosing the right video model

Seedance 2.0 is the best all-rounder for music videos. It generates videos with native audio and produces the most coherent visual storytelling. Best for vocal tracks and conceptual videos. Cost: ~₹12/second.

Veo 3.1 Fast (Google) produces cinematic quality at reasonable cost. Native audio included. Excellent for scenic or atmospheric music videos without lip-sync requirements. Cost: ~₹21/second.

Wan 2.7 is an open-source model that's significantly cheaper. Quality is lower but it's ideal for long songs where covering every second would be prohibitively expensive. Cost: ~₹3/second.

Beat detection explained

The Rhythm Cuts strategy uses the Web Audio API to analyse your audio for energy peaks. The algorithm computes a spectral flux (rate of change of energy across frequency bands) over time, then finds local maxima above a dynamic threshold. These peaks typically correspond to kick drums, snare hits, or major beat drops — exactly where a cut in a music video feels natural.

Privacy and data

Your Replicate API key is stored only in your browser's localStorage and is transmitted only to Replicate via our CORS proxy — which is a dumb pass-through that reads nothing. The audio upload to tmpfiles.org is anonymous; no account is required and files expire in 1 hour. The video stitching happens entirely in your browser using ffmpeg.wasm — the assembled video never leaves your device until you download it.

Frequently asked questions

How long can my audio track be?

There's no hard limit, but longer tracks mean more clips and higher cost. A 3-minute song using "Cover every second" with Veo 3.1 Fast would cost roughly $45 — so we recommend using "Rhythm cuts" or "Single long take" for long tracks. We'll always show you the cost estimate before you start.

Why does it need to upload my audio to tmpfiles.org?

Replicate's video models need a publicly accessible URL for audio input — they can't receive files directly from your browser. tmpfiles.org provides anonymous temporary hosting (1-hour TTL, no account needed). We use this as the default and fall back to file.io. You can also paste a direct URL if your audio is already hosted somewhere.

What is ffmpeg.wasm and why does it download 30 MB?

ffmpeg.wasm is a WebAssembly port of the ffmpeg video processing library — the same tool professional video editors use, running entirely in your browser. The first time you use the stitching feature, it downloads about 30 MB of compiled WebAssembly code. After that, it's cached by your browser indefinitely. The benefit: all video processing happens locally, with no server required.

Can I use this with a song I generated on the Music Studio page?

Yes. Switch to the "From Music Studio" tab in Step 1, and your recent Suno generations will appear as selectable options. Their audio URLs are already public (hosted by Suno), so no re-upload is needed.

The video doesn't sync well to my music. What can I do?

Use "Rhythm cuts" mode — it places cuts at detected beat peaks, which creates the most natural music-video feel. Also try adjusting your concept prompt to include camera movement language like "quick cuts", "camera panning left" or "slow zoom" to make individual clips feel more dynamic.

Can I cancel a generation in progress?

Yes. Click the Cancel button during generation. This sends a cancel request to Replicate for any in-flight predictions. Note that you may still be billed for time already consumed by predictions that were running when you cancelled.
