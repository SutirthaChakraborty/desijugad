AI video generation at direct API cost

Video generation is the most compute-intensive thing you can do with AI — which is why most tools charge subscription prices or clip you off after a few seconds. Desi Jugad Video Studio connects your Replicate account directly, so you pay exactly what Replicate charges: typically $0.10–$0.50 per second of generated video.

That sounds expensive, but consider: a single 8-second clip with Veo 3.1 Fast costs $2. With Wan 2.7 (open source), the same clip is about $0.32. For music videos, short social clips, and B-roll, the economics are solid — especially compared to stock footage licensing.

Which model for which use case?

Veo 3.1 is Google's flagship. It generates native audio (music, dialogue, sound effects) alongside video, and handles lip-sync well. Use it for talking-head videos, product demos, and anything where audio matters. Seedance 2.0 has the best lip-sync of any model as of April 2026 — ideal for music videos with vocals. Kling 3.0 handles multi-shot sequences (you describe a scene, it generates multiple camera angles). Wan 2.7 is open source, cheap, and generates 1080p — great for B-roll and establishing shots where budget matters.

Image-to-video explained

Upload a still image and describe how you want it to move. Wan 2.7 I2V also accepts a "last frame" image — specify where the clip ends as well as where it starts, and the model generates the motion in between. This is useful for smooth transitions between scenes in a music video.

Frequently asked questions

How long does video generation take?

Typically 2–8 minutes depending on model and duration. Veo 3.1 Fast and Wan 2.7 are on the faster end. The stream log shows live status from Replicate while you wait.

Can I generate video longer than 15 seconds?

Most models max out at 8–15 seconds per generation. For longer pieces, use the Music Video Maker page which chains multiple clips and stitches them together.

What does "native audio" mean?

Models marked 🔊 generate audio (ambient sound, music, or speech) alongside the video frames. You don't need to add audio separately. The quality varies by model — Veo 3.1 and Seedance 2.0 are the strongest.

What resolution can I generate?

Most models support 720p and 1080p. 480p is available as a cheaper option. Not all models support all resolutions — unavailable options are grayed out based on the selected model.

Why is the cost estimate shown before generating?

Because video can get expensive fast. A 15-second Veo 3.1 clip costs $7.50. We want you to know before you click, not after. You'll see a warning if estimated cost exceeds $5.
