Write long-form content with the best AI models — at direct API cost

Desi Jugad AI Writer connects you directly to frontier LLMs on Replicate. No subscription, no per-word pricing, no watermarks — you pay Replicate at their published rate, and the content is yours.

Which model should you choose?

Claude Opus 4.7 (via Replicate) is the best all-around writer. It understands nuance, maintains consistent voice throughout long pieces, follows complex instructions, and rarely produces generic filler text. Recommended for anything you plan to actually publish. Cost: roughly $0.015 per 1,000 output tokens (~750 words).

Llama 3.3 70B is Meta's open-source model and the best free-tier option. Quality is very good for structured content, listicles, and technical writing. Significantly cheaper than Claude for long-form work. Recommended if you're producing large volumes of content.

Mistral Large is efficient and produces clean prose. It's especially good at formal and technical content. A solid middle ground in both quality and cost.

How long-form mode works

For 5,000+ word pieces, the writer uses a two-pass approach: first, it generates a detailed outline with section headings and bullet points. You can review and edit this outline before committing to a full write. Then it writes each section individually, keeping the previous sections in context so the piece reads as one coherent document. Each call is typically 1,500–2,000 words, so a 10,000-word piece makes 5–7 API calls.

Export options

Your generated content can be copied to clipboard, downloaded as a Markdown file (.md), or exported as a Word document (.docx) using the docx library. You can also send it to Desi Jugad's Markdown-to-HTML converter to preview it as a formatted web page.

Privacy

Your prompts and generated content are not stored on any Desi Jugad server. The API calls go from your browser to Replicate via the CORS proxy (a dumb pass-through). Your Replicate key is stored only in your browser's localStorage. Generated text lives only in the browser until you choose to copy or download it.

Frequently asked questions

How long can the generated content be?

In single-pass mode, up to about 3,000 words depending on the model. In long-form (outline) mode, there's no hard limit — the tool generates sections sequentially, so you can get 10,000+ words given enough API credit. Typical 10,000-word generation takes 5–7 minutes.

Can I edit the content after it's generated?

Yes — the output area is fully editable (contenteditable). Click anywhere and type. The export buttons will pick up your edits.

What is the .docx export for?

It exports your content as a Microsoft Word document using the docx.js library — entirely in your browser. You can open it in Word, Google Docs, or LibreOffice and continue editing or formatting it.

How much does it cost to generate a 1,000-word article?

With Claude Opus 4.7, roughly $0.015 (about ₹1.25). With Llama 3.3 70B, under $0.003 (about ₹0.25). This is billed directly to your Replicate account.

Does it support Hindi or other Indian languages?

Claude Opus 4.7 and Llama 3.3 70B both support Hindi. Simply specify in your prompt: "Write this in Hindi" or "Write in Hinglish." Quality in Hindi is reasonably good for both models, though Claude generally produces more natural prose.

Can I stop generation partway through?

Yes. Click the Stop button at any time. Content written so far will remain in the output area and is fully editable and exportable. Mid-stream stops may leave the last sentence incomplete — just edit it manually.
