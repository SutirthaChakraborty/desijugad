The subtitle format you need, depending on where it's playing

SRT (SubRip) is the most universal subtitle format — supported by VLC, Plex, most TVs, and every video editor. WebVTT (.vtt) is the web-native format used by HTML5 <video> elements and YouTube's caption upload. They carry the same data but use slightly different syntax, so converting between them takes two seconds — once you know the rules.

What's different between SRT and VTT?

  • VTT starts with a WEBVTT header line. SRT doesn't.
  • SRT uses commas as the millisecond separator (00:00:01,500). VTT uses dots (00:00:01.500).
  • VTT supports cue styling, positioning, and voice tags. SRT is plain text with optional HTML-ish formatting.

Bonus: timing shift

If your subtitles are 1.5 seconds late the whole way through, enter -1.5 in the shift field and every timestamp will be adjusted. Positive numbers push subtitles later; negative numbers pull them earlier.

FAQ

Does this handle multi-line subtitles?

Yes. Multi-line cues, blank lines within cues, and Unicode characters are all preserved correctly.

Can I open large subtitle files?

Yes — full-length movies (2,000+ cues) convert instantly.

Will basic formatting (italics, colors) be preserved?

Standard HTML-like tags (<i>, <b>, <u>) are carried across both ways. More exotic VTT styling is dropped when converting to SRT since SRT doesn't support it.
