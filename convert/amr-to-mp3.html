Why AMR is tricky in browsers

AMR (Adaptive Multi-Rate) is a speech codec developed for GSM mobile phones. It's widely used in Android voice recorder apps and WhatsApp voice notes. The problem: AMR decoding requires a licensed codec that most desktop browser vendors haven't included due to patent restrictions.

Where AMR works in-browser:

  • Chrome on Android — Has native AMR-NB/AMR-WB support. Open this page on your Android phone for the best results.
  • Desktop Chrome/Edge/Firefox — No AMR support. Conversion will fail with a decode error.
  • Safari on iOS/macOS — Limited AMR support, may work on newer versions.

If browser conversion doesn't work, these desktop tools handle AMR reliably: VLC Media Player (free), Audacity with FFmpeg plugin (free), or FFmpeg command line.

FAQ

Why does it say "decoding failed" on my computer?

Desktop Chrome and Firefox don't include the AMR decoder. This is a browser limitation, not a tool bug. Open this page on your Android phone using Chrome, or use VLC on desktop to open and re-export the AMR file.

My WhatsApp voice notes are AMR. How do I convert them?

WhatsApp voice messages are stored as .opus files on Android (not AMR) — try the Audio Converter. On older Android, they may be .amr files in WhatsApp/Media/WhatsApp Voice Notes/. Use Android Chrome to open this page and convert.

What bitrate should I use for AMR voice?

AMR records at 8 kHz bandwidth. 64 kbps MP3 is more than enough for voice quality — higher bitrates won't improve the audio since the source is already 8 kHz narrow-band. Save space with 64 kbps.
