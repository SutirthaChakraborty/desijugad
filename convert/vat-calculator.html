Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add VAT to a price?

Multiply the net price by (1 + rate/100). For UK 20% VAT: £100 × 1.20 = £120 total. The VAT amount is £20.

How do I remove VAT from a price (reverse VAT)?

Divide the VAT-inclusive price by (1 + rate/100). For UK 20% VAT: £120 ÷ 1.20 = £100 net. VAT amount = £120 - £100 = £20.

What is the difference between VAT and sales tax?

VAT is collected at every stage of the supply chain (producer, wholesaler, retailer). Each stage pays VAT on value added. US sales tax is only collected once at the final point of sale. VAT is more prevalent globally; sales tax is mainly used in the USA.

Which countries have the highest VAT rates in Europe?

Hungary (27%) has the highest standard VAT in Europe, followed by Sweden, Denmark, Croatia, and Norway (all 25%). Luxembourg has the lowest at 17%.

Does the USA have VAT?

No. The USA does not have a federal VAT. Instead, most US states levy sales tax (ranging from 0% in Oregon/Montana to 10.25% in some California counties). The US is one of the few developed economies without a national VAT.
