How video compression works

This tool re-encodes your video using the H.264 codec with a CRF (Constant Rate Factor) value you choose. Lower CRF = better quality but larger file. Higher CRF = more compression, smaller file. The preset fast is used for good speed on all devices.

FAQ

How much can I reduce the file size?

Results vary by source material. Uncompressed or poorly-encoded videos see the biggest reduction. A 100 MB iPhone video might compress to 25–40 MB at "Small" quality with minimal visible difference.

Will re-encoding reduce quality?

Yes, slightly. Every re-encode of a lossy format introduces some quality loss. The "High" setting (CRF 18) is nearly transparent — most people can't tell the difference.
