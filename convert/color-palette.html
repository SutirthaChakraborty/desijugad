Color harmony types

  • Complementary — Colors opposite on the color wheel. High contrast, vibrant.
  • Triadic — Three colors evenly spaced (120° apart). Balanced and vivid.
  • Analogous — Colors adjacent on the wheel. Harmonious and pleasing.
  • Split-complementary — Base color + two colors adjacent to its complement. Less tension than pure complementary.
  • Monochromatic — Different shades, tints, and tones of a single hue.
