How in-browser video conversion works

This tool loads FFmpeg WebAssembly — a full port of the industry-standard FFmpeg encoder compiled to run inside your browser. All encoding happens on your CPU via WebAssembly threads. Files never leave your device.

Which formats are supported?

Input: any format FFmpeg supports — MP4, WebM, MOV, AVI, MKV, FLV, OGV, M4V, 3GP, and more. Output: MP4 (H.264), WebM (VP9), MOV, AVI, MKV, OGV.

FAQ

Why does the first conversion take longer?

FFmpeg WebAssembly (~30 MB) is downloaded and compiled once per session. Subsequent conversions in the same tab are fast because the engine stays loaded.

Is there a file size limit?

No hard limit — it depends on your device RAM. A 1 GB video needs roughly 1–2 GB of free RAM. Most modern laptops and phones handle files up to 500 MB easily.

Which format should I choose?

Use MP4 for widest compatibility (phones, TVs, social media). Use WebM for web embedding (smaller size, open format). Use MOV for Apple devices and editing software.
