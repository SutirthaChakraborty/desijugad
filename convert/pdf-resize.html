How PDF page resizing works

This tool creates a new PDF with pages set to your chosen dimensions. For each original page, the content is scaled proportionally to fit the new canvas, preserving aspect ratio and adding equal margins if needed.

Preset sizes

A4 (210×297 mm) is the most common international standard. Letter (216×279 mm) is standard in the US. Legal (216×356 mm) is used for legal documents. A3 is double-A4; A5 is half-A4.

Custom dimensions

Enter any width and height in millimetres. Useful for making PDFs fit unusual display or print requirements, such as digital notebooks or presentation slides.

FAQ

Does resizing affect text searchability?

No. PDF text vectors scale with the page and remain fully searchable and selectable after resizing.

Can I resize only some pages?

This tool resizes all pages uniformly. To resize specific pages, use our Extract Pages tool to pull those pages out, resize the extracted PDF, then merge back with PDF Merge.

Will the PDF look different after resizing?

Content scales proportionally so text, images, and layout appear the same visually. If aspect ratios differ significantly, there may be small whitespace margins around the content.
