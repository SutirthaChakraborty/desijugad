How audio extraction works

FFmpeg WebAssembly reads your video file and strips the video stream entirely, keeping only the audio track. For MP3/AAC output, the audio is re-encoded at your chosen bitrate. For WAV/FLAC, the audio is decoded losslessly. No re-encoding of video means extraction is fast.

FAQ

Which format should I pick?

MP3 — best compatibility everywhere. AAC — better quality at same bitrate, great for Apple devices. WAV/FLAC — lossless, large files, best for music production. OGG — open format, great for web/games.

Will the audio quality match the original?

If the source video has a high-quality audio track and you choose 320 kbps or WAV/FLAC, yes. Choosing lower bitrate introduces compression. The original video audio quality is the ceiling.

Can I extract from YouTube videos?

This tool only works with files already on your device. To use it with online videos, first download the video to your device, then extract the audio here.
