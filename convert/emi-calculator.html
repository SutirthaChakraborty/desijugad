How EMI is calculated

EMI = P × r × (1+r)n / ((1+r)n − 1) — where P is the principal, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the total number of months.

Tips to reduce EMI

  • Make a larger down payment to reduce principal.
  • Negotiate a lower interest rate — even 0.5% less saves lakhs on a home loan.
  • Prepay whenever you have a surplus; principal prepayment cuts interest drastically.
  • Choose a longer tenure to lower EMI, but note the total interest increases.

FAQ

Is this calculator accurate?

Yes — it uses the standard reducing-balance formula used by all banks and NBFCs in India.

Does this work for home, car, and personal loans?

Yes — the formula is the same for all fixed-rate EMI loans. Just enter your loan's rate and tenure.
