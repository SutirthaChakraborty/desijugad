Tax & Deductions Guide by Country

🇬🇧 United Kingdom

Personal allowance: £12,570/year (tax-free). Basic rate: 20% on £12,570–£50,270. Higher rate: 40% on £50,271–£125,140. Additional rate: 45% above £125,140. National Insurance: 8% on £12,570–£50,270, 2% above.

🇺🇸 United States

Federal standard deduction: $14,600 (single, 2025). Tax brackets: 10%→12%→22%→24%→32%→35%→37%. Plus Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%). State taxes vary 0%–13%.

🇮🇳 India (New Regime)

Tax-free up to ₹12 lakh (after rebate u/s 87A). Slabs: 5% (₹3–7L), 10% (₹7–10L), 15% (₹10–12L), 20% (₹12–15L), 30% above ₹15L. Standard deduction ₹75,000.

🇳🇬 Nigeria

PAYE: 7% (up to ₦600K) → 11% → 15% → 19% → 21% → 24% (above ₦3.2M). Pension: 8% employee contribution. NHF: 2.5%. Tax-free threshold: ₦800,000/year (CRA + reliefs).

🇰🇪 Kenya

PAYE: 10% (KSh 0–24K/month) → 25% → 30% (above KSh 57,334). Personal relief: KSh 2,400/month. NHIF: up to KSh 1,700/month. NSSF: up to KSh 2,160/month. Housing Levy: 1.5%.

🇵🇰 Pakistan (FY 2025-26)

Tax-free: up to PKR 600,000. Then: 5% → 15% → 20% → 25% → 30% → 35% (above PKR 6M). EOBI: 1% employee contribution. Provincial social security: 1–2%.

🇧🇩 Bangladesh

Tax-free: up to BDT 350,000 (men), BDT 400,000 (women/senior). Rates: 5% → 10% → 15% → 20% → 25% → 30%. Minimum tax applies in 11 districts.

🇦🇪 UAE

UAE has no personal income tax. All salary is tax-free for employees. Corporate tax (9%) applies to companies, not employees. No deductions from your paycheck.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is gross salary vs net salary?

Gross salary is your total pay before any deductions. Net salary (take-home pay) is what you receive after income tax, social security, pension, and other deductions are removed.

How accurate is this salary calculator?

This tool uses simplified flat-rate tax estimates for quick comparison. For exact figures — especially with progressive tax brackets, state/provincial taxes, or complex deductions — consult a tax professional or your country's official tax portal.

Does the UAE have income tax on salary?

No. The UAE has no personal income tax. Employees keep their entire gross salary. A 9% corporate tax was introduced in 2023 for businesses, but it does not affect employee take-home pay.

What is a typical income tax rate in Nigeria?

Nigeria uses PAYE (Pay As You Earn) with progressive rates from 7% to 24%. Most middle-income earners (₦2M–₦5M annual) pay an effective rate of 12–18%. The Consolidated Relief Allowance significantly reduces taxable income.
