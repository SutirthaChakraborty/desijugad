Smart image compression, explained

This compressor uses the browser's native Canvas API with controllable JPEG/WebP quality encoding, plus optional dimension scaling for maximum size reduction. No server involved — everything processes locally.

When to compress images

  • Web pages — every 100 KB you shave off means faster load times and better SEO scores.
  • Email attachments — keep photos under 1 MB so they land in inboxes, not spam folders.
  • WhatsApp / social media — compressing first prevents the platform's own auto-compression from destroying quality on your terms.
  • Storage — halve your photo library size without visible quality change.

FAQ

How much can I compress without visible quality loss?

For most photographs, 70–85% quality is visually identical to the original. For graphics with sharp edges (logos, screenshots), 85–95% is safer. Use the quality slider to preview before downloading.

Does compressing a PNG make it a JPG?

No — this tool preserves the output format. Compressing a PNG keeps it PNG. However, if your PNG contains photos (not flat graphics), converting it to JPG or WebP first and then compressing will yield dramatically smaller files.

Are my images uploaded anywhere?

Never. Everything runs in JavaScript inside your browser tab. When you close the tab, nothing remains.
