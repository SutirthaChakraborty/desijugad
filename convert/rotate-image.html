About image rotation

This tool uses the browser's Canvas API with ctx.translate and ctx.rotate to rotate images precisely. For 90° and 270° rotations, the output canvas dimensions are swapped (width ↔ height) to avoid cropping. For custom angles, the canvas expands to fit the entire rotated image.

When to rotate images

  • Camera EXIF fix — Some phones store photos with rotation metadata. Rotating corrects the visual orientation permanently.
  • Document scanning — Scanned PDFs and images often come in at the wrong angle.
  • Creative effects — Angled images for social media banners, collages, and presentations.

FAQ

What is the difference between clockwise and counter-clockwise rotation?

90° Clockwise (CW) rotates the image to the right — the top becomes the right side. 90° Counter-Clockwise (CCW, or 270° CW) rotates left — the top becomes the left side.

Does the canvas crop the image for custom angles?

No. For any custom angle, the output canvas is automatically enlarged to fit the full rotated image with transparent or white padding, so nothing is cropped.

Are my images uploaded to a server?

Never. All processing runs in JavaScript in your browser. Files are not sent anywhere.
