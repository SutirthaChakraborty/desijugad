How OCR works in your browser

Tesseract is the world's most popular open-source OCR engine. Tesseract.js is a WebAssembly port that runs the full engine directly in your browser — no Python, no server, no install.

First run takes longer

On your first OCR, Tesseract downloads a language model (~5 MB for English). This is cached by your browser, so subsequent scans are nearly instant. No model data is ever sent to our servers.

Tips for best accuracy

  • Use high-resolution images (300 DPI+) for printed text.
  • Ensure good contrast between text and background.
  • Crop tightly around the text region before uploading.
  • For mixed-language documents, select the dominant language.

FAQ

Can it read handwritten text?

Tesseract was designed for printed text. Handwriting recognition is limited — neat block handwriting may partially work, but cursive is mostly unreliable. For handwriting, a cloud AI model would perform better.

Is the language model downloaded each session?

No — Tesseract.js caches language models in IndexedDB. After the first load, they're stored in your browser and reused automatically.

Does it work with PDFs?

Not directly. Use our PDF to Images tool first to extract pages, then run OCR on each image.
