About the MP4 format

MP4 (MPEG-4 Part 14, file extension .mp4) is the dominant video container format of the modern era. It is not a codec itself but a container — a wrapper that holds video, audio, subtitles, and metadata streams together in a single file.

The most common combination is H.264 video (also known as AVC) with AAC audio. H.264 offers an excellent balance of quality and compression efficiency, and since 2010 virtually every device — smartphone, laptop, smart TV, gaming console — includes hardware decoders for it, enabling smooth playback without draining the CPU or battery. Newer MP4 files may use H.265 (HEVC) which offers 40–50 % better compression at equivalent quality but requires more modern hardware to decode efficiently.

MP4 is accepted by every major platform: YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, WhatsApp, and nearly all video editing applications. When in doubt about compatibility, converting any video format to MP4 is almost always the right choice.

FAQ

What makes MP4 the most widely supported video format?

MP4 (MPEG-4 Part 14) is a container format that wraps H.264 or H.265 video with AAC audio. These codecs are hardware-accelerated on virtually every device made since 2010 — phones, smart TVs, gaming consoles, laptops. This universal hardware support means MP4 plays smoothly everywhere without software decoding overhead.

How do I convert MOV to MP4?

MOV files from iPhones and Mac cameras use the same H.264 or HEVC video as MP4 — just in Apple's QuickTime container. Converting MOV to MP4 is essentially a container remux with no quality loss. Use our MOV to MP4 converter above.

Will compressing an MP4 reduce quality?

Yes. Video compression is lossy — reducing the bitrate removes detail from the video. The goal is to find the bitrate where the quality loss is imperceptible for your use case. For social media, 2–5 Mbps is usually sufficient for 1080p. For archival, use the highest bitrate that fits your storage budget.
