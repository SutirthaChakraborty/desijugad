Why you need to convert HEIC files

HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is the default photo format on every iPhone since 2017. It produces smaller files with better quality than JPG — but there's a catch: most non-Apple software can't open it. Windows Photos, older versions of Adobe software, most web browsers, WhatsApp on older devices, and countless email clients all fail to display HEIC images.

This tool solves that in about two seconds. Drop your HEIC files, pick an output format, and save the result. No account, no upload, no size limit.

How our HEIC converter works

The entire conversion happens inside your web browser using a WebAssembly port of libheif — the same open-source library used by major image tools. Your files are loaded into memory on your device, processed locally, and re-saved in the format you choose. Nothing is ever sent to our servers, because we don't have any conversion servers.

Because your CPU does the work directly, a single photo typically converts in under a second. A batch of 50 photos finishes in about 20–40 seconds depending on your device.

Why JPG is usually the right choice

  • Universal compatibility — every browser, messaging app, email client, and photo viewer supports JPG.
  • Smaller sharing sizes — JPG compresses photographs efficiently for social media and WhatsApp.
  • Printer-friendly — almost every photo printing service expects JPG input.

If you're editing photos in professional software and want lossless quality, choose PNG. If you're publishing to a modern website and want the best file-size-to-quality ratio, choose WebP.

Frequently asked questions

Are my photos uploaded anywhere?

No. Desi Jugad has no conversion server. Everything happens inside your browser using JavaScript and WebAssembly. You can confirm this by opening your browser's developer tools, switching to the Network tab, and converting a file — no outgoing file upload will appear.

Is there a file size limit?

The only limit is your device's available memory. On a modern phone or laptop, HEIC files of 100MB+ convert without issue. Extremely large batches may slow down your browser tab briefly.

Does this work on iPhone and Android?

Yes. Any modern mobile browser (Safari 14+, Chrome, Firefox) can run the converter. On iPhone you'll typically pick the HEIC file from your Photos app via the Files picker.

Do I lose any quality when converting?

JPG and WebP are lossy formats, so a tiny amount of quality is lost at the default setting (0.92). For most photos this is visually indistinguishable. If you need zero quality loss, choose PNG — it is lossless.

Why convert HEIC at all — can't I just email it?

Sometimes yes, but frequently the receiving device can't display HEIC or your recipient's software can't edit it. Converting to JPG eliminates the compatibility gamble.

Will metadata (EXIF, location) be kept?

Basic orientation metadata is preserved so your photos don't appear rotated. Sensitive metadata like GPS coordinates is stripped by default, which is safer for sharing publicly.
