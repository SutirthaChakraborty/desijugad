New Regime vs Old Regime — which is better?

The new regime (default from FY 2024-25) offers lower slab rates but most deductions (80C, HRA, home loan interest) are not available. The old regime has higher slab rates but allows all deductions. If you have significant deductions (80C + HRA + home loan), the old regime may save more. Otherwise, the new regime is usually better.

FAQ

Is the ₹12 lakh threshold under new regime correct?

Yes. Under the new regime for FY 2025-26, income up to ₹12,00,000 is effectively zero tax due to the rebate under Section 87A (₹60,000 rebate). Above ₹12L, normal slab rates apply.

Does this include surcharge and cess?

Yes — this calculator includes the 4% Health and Education Cess on tax payable. Surcharge (for income above ₹50L) is included at applicable rates.

Is this calculation accurate?

This is an estimate for salaried individuals. The actual tax liability may differ based on specific income sources, exemptions not covered here, and provisions applicable to your situation. Consult a CA for exact calculations.
