How to use the color picker

  • EyeDropper (Pick from Screen) — Works in Chrome 95+ and Edge. Click the button, then click any pixel anywhere on your screen — even outside the browser.
  • Image upload — Upload a PNG, JPG, or WebP and click any spot on the image to sample that pixel's color exactly.
  • Gradient canvas — A full-spectrum gradient lets you visually explore and click any hue/saturation/lightness.
  • Native picker — The browser's built-in color picker for quick selection.

FAQ

Why doesn't the EyeDropper button work in Firefox or Safari?

The EyeDropper API is currently supported in Chrome 95+ and Edge 95+. Firefox and Safari do not yet support it. As a fallback, upload your image and use the canvas picker instead.

Is my image uploaded to a server?

No. Your image is loaded entirely in the browser using JavaScript's FileReader and Canvas API. Nothing leaves your device.

What is the difference between HEX, RGB, and HSL?

HEX is the 6-digit web format (#RRGGBB). RGB gives you the red, green, and blue channel values from 0–255. HSL (Hue, Saturation, Lightness) is more intuitive for designers — hue is the color angle (0–360°), saturation is the color intensity, and lightness controls brightness.
