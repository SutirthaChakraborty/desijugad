What is the Pomodoro Technique?

Developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s, the Pomodoro Technique uses a timer to break work into intervals — traditionally 25 minutes — separated by short breaks. The name "Pomodoro" (Italian for tomato) comes from the tomato-shaped kitchen timer Cirillo used as a university student.

Research shows that regular breaks improve mental agility, reduce fatigue, and increase overall productivity.

FAQ

Why 25 minutes?

25 minutes is long enough to make meaningful progress but short enough to stay focused without mental fatigue. You can customize the duration — some people prefer 45-minute or 50-minute sessions.

Does the timer keep running if I switch tabs?

Yes — the timer uses setInterval which continues even when the tab is hidden. You'll hear the alert sound when the session ends.
