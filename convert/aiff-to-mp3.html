About AIFF and why convert to MP3

AIFF (Audio Interchange File Format) is Apple's uncompressed lossless audio format — a direct equivalent to WAV on Windows. It stores raw PCM audio, which means large file sizes (a 3-minute stereo AIFF at 44.1 kHz is ~30 MB). Converting to MP3 reduces this by 10x while maintaining near-perfect audio quality at 320 kbps.

  • Logic Pro / GarageBand exports — These often default to AIFF. Convert to MP3 for distribution.
  • Pro Tools sessions — Audio tracks are often AIFF. Convert for sharing or streaming.
  • Legacy iTunes library — Old CD rips may be stored as AIFF. Convert for mobile use.

Browser note: AIFF decoding works best in Safari and Chrome. Firefox may not support AIFF natively. If decoding fails, try Safari on macOS.

FAQ

Does converting AIFF to MP3 lose quality?

Yes — AIFF is lossless and MP3 is lossy. At 320 kbps, the quality difference is imperceptible in double-blind tests for most people. For studio archival, keep your AIFF originals and only convert copies for distribution or streaming.

Why are AIFF files so large?

AIFF stores uncompressed 16-bit or 24-bit PCM audio. A stereo 44.1 kHz 16-bit track uses about 10 MB per minute. A 320 kbps MP3 of the same material uses about 2.4 MB per minute — roughly a 4x reduction.

My AIFF file failed to decode. What do I do?

Some AIFF files use 24-bit or 32-bit float samples that older browser decoders struggle with. Try opening this page in Chrome or Safari — both have robust AIFF support. Alternatively, try the full Audio Converter.
