About the EPUB format

EPUB (Electronic Publication) is the open standard for ebooks maintained by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), succeeding the older OEB (Open eBook) format. EPUB 3, the current version, is based on HTML5, CSS3, and SVG — essentially a small website packaged into a ZIP file with an .epub extension.

An EPUB contains: an OPF metadata file (title, author, language, table of contents), HTML/XHTML content files for chapters, a CSS stylesheet, images and fonts, and an NCX or NAV file for navigation. The content is reflowable by default — text adapts to the reader's font size, screen size, and orientation, unlike PDF which has a fixed page layout.

EPUB is supported by: Apple Books, Google Play Books, Kobo, Kindle (via Send to Kindle), Adobe Digital Editions, and every major ebook reader device. Amazon Kindle historically used its own MOBI/AZW format but added direct EPUB support in 2022. Project Gutenberg, Standard Ebooks, and most library systems distribute books in EPUB format.

EPUB 3 supports: audio and video, scripted interactivity, MathML for mathematical content, accessibility (ARIA roles), right-to-left scripts, and vertical text for East Asian publications.

FAQ

What is an EPUB file?

EPUB (Electronic Publication) is the standard ebook format maintained by the W3C. It is a ZIP archive containing HTML/XHTML content files, a CSS stylesheet, images, fonts, and metadata files. The content reflows to fit any screen size, unlike PDFs which have fixed page layouts. EPUB 3 is the current standard, supporting audio, video, interactivity, and MathML.

Why convert EPUB to PDF?

PDFs have a fixed layout ideal for printing or sharing when you need the document to look exactly the same on every device. EPUBs are reflowable — text resizes and reflows for different screen sizes. Convert EPUB to PDF when you need to print an ebook, share it with someone who doesn't have an ebook reader, or submit it to a service that requires PDF.

Can I read DRM-protected EPUB files?

No. DRM-protected EPUBs purchased from stores like Amazon, Kobo, or Google Play are encrypted and cannot be opened without the DRM layer. Our EPUB viewer works only with DRM-free EPUB files — such as books from Project Gutenberg, Standard Ebooks, or your own authored content.
