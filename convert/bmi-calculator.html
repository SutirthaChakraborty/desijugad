What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) = weight (kg) ÷ height² (m²). It's a screening tool, not a diagnostic measure. A high BMI may indicate excess body fat, but athletes with high muscle mass can have high BMI without health risks. Always consult a doctor for health advice.

Note for South Asians: Research suggests South Asians (including Indians) have higher health risks at lower BMI values. Consider 23 as the upper limit of normal and 27.5 as the overweight threshold.

FAQ

Is BMI accurate for everyone?

BMI doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, age, sex, or ethnicity. It's a population-level screening tool, not a personal health diagnosis. Athletes may have "overweight" BMI despite being very healthy.
