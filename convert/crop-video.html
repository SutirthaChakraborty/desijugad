Spatial cropping vs time trimming

This tool crops the frame — it removes the top/bottom/sides of the video image. Use it to:

  • Change aspect ratio — Convert landscape 16:9 video to 9:16 portrait for Instagram Reels or TikTok.
  • Remove letterboxing/pillarboxing — Crop out black bars from videos.
  • Focus on a subject — Zoom into the center of a wide shot.
  • Square crop for Instagram — Use the 1:1 preset for feed posts.

Need to cut the video shorter? Use the Video Trimmer instead.

Export note: The output is WebM on Chrome/Firefox, MP4 on Safari. Encoding is real-time — a 2-minute video takes ~2 minutes to process.

FAQ

How do I use the crop tool?

1. Drop or choose a video file. 2. A frame preview appears — click and drag on it to draw your crop rectangle. 3. Optionally click a preset (16:9, 1:1, etc.) to lock the aspect ratio. 4. Click "Export cropped video" — the video will play in the background and record the cropped view. 5. Download the result.

Why does it take as long as the video duration?

The tool uses MediaRecorder, which captures the canvas in real time as the video plays. There's no "fast" path for this in a browser without FFmpeg. For batch or fast cropping, use a desktop app.

Does the audio stay in the cropped video?

Yes! The audio track is captured from the video element and added to the MediaRecorder stream. The output file contains both the cropped video and the original audio.
