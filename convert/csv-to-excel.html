From plain text to a real spreadsheet

A CSV is just text — readable by any program, but it doesn't carry formatting, formulas, column widths, or multiple sheets. Converting to .xlsx gives you a real Excel workbook your recipient can open, edit, and share without conversion quirks.

What it handles

  • Quoted fields containing commas, newlines, and double-quotes.
  • Auto-detection of common delimiters (comma, semicolon, tab, pipe).
  • UTF-8 encoding so Unicode text and emoji survive intact.
  • Multiple CSVs → one workbook, each CSV becomes a named sheet.

FAQ

Does it preserve numbers vs text?

Yes — numeric-looking values become numbers in Excel. Text like "007" stays text so you don't lose leading zeroes.

Can Excel actually open the result?

The output is a real Office Open XML file, identical to what Excel produces. It opens in Excel, Numbers, Google Sheets, and LibreOffice Calc.

Is my data sent anywhere?

No. The whole thing runs on SheetJS inside your browser. Network tab will show zero uploads.
