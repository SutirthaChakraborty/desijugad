How MOV to MP4 conversion works in-browser

The browser loads your MOV file into a hidden <video> element and plays it while a MediaRecorder captures the video stream from a <canvas>. The captured stream is muxed into a WebM or MP4 container depending on your browser's support.

  • Chrome/Edge — Output is WebM (VP8/VP9 codec). Playable everywhere modern.
  • Safari — Output is MP4 (H.264 codec). For genuine MP4, use Safari on macOS.
  • Processing time — Real-time encode: a 2-minute video takes ~2 minutes to process.

FAQ

Why is the output WebM and not MP4?

Browser MediaRecorder on Chrome/Firefox defaults to WebM/VP8 since it's a fully open format with hardware acceleration. Safari encodes to MP4. Both formats are widely compatible on modern devices. You can also use the Video Converter for proper MP4 output in any browser.

Can I convert iPhone HEVC MOV files?

HEVC (H.265) encoded MOV files from iPhone may not be decodable by Chrome. Safari on macOS handles them natively. For cross-browser HEVC conversion, use the Video Converter.

Why does it take so long?

The MediaRecorder approach processes video in real-time — a 5-minute video takes ~5 minutes to convert. This is a limitation of in-browser conversion without FFmpeg. For faster batch conversion, see Video Converter.
