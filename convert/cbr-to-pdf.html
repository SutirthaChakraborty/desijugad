What are CBZ and CBR files?

CBZ (Comic Book ZIP) and CBR (Comic Book RAR) are archive formats used to distribute digital comics. They contain a sequence of image files — one per page — sorted alphabetically or numerically. Most comic readers (MangaReader, CDisplay, Calibre) support these formats natively.

How the conversion works

This tool uses JSZip to decompress the CBZ archive, extracts all image files, sorts them alphabetically (which matches page order in standard CBZ files), then uses jsPDF to create a PDF where each image fills one page.

Page orientation

Each page is sized to match the image dimensions — portrait pages stay portrait, landscape pages stay landscape. Standard comic pages are typically around 1024×1536 px.

FAQ

Can I read the resulting PDF on a Kindle or e-reader?

Yes. Most modern e-readers support PDF. For best results on Kindle, consider using Calibre on your desktop to also convert to MOBI or AZW3 format for better reflow.

My pages are out of order in the PDF

The tool sorts filenames alphabetically. If your CBZ uses numeric filenames like 1.jpg, 2.jpg, 10.jpg, alphabetical sorting will put page 10 before page 2. Well-made CBZ files use zero-padded names like 001.jpg, 002.jpg, 010.jpg to avoid this.

Does this work with CBZ files that contain WebP images?

Yes, modern browsers support WebP natively in canvas, so WebP pages inside a CBZ will be converted correctly.

How do I convert CBR to CBZ on desktop?

Use 7-Zip (free) — open the CBR, select all files, and add them to a new ZIP archive. Then rename the .zip to .cbz. Alternatively, Calibre has a built-in CBR→CBZ conversion tool.
