About us

A file converter that doesn't touch your files.

Desi Jugad is an independent project built in India by a small team that got tired of ad-stuffed converter websites that upload your files to servers you don't know. We started with a simple hypothesis: modern browsers are powerful enough to do the conversion themselves, and removing the server is better for everyone.

The privacy promise

Every tool on this site runs entirely in your browser. When you drop a file onto Desi Jugad, the file stays on your device. The conversion happens in your own memory, using JavaScript and WebAssembly. Nothing is uploaded, because there is no server to upload it to.

You can verify this any time — open your browser's Network tab and watch. You will not see your file leaving.

Made in India, built for the world

We're based in India. That's where the servers are, that's where the team is, and that's where we pay taxes. We believe the best internet tools should come from everywhere — not just Silicon Valley — and we're proud to be part of a growing wave of Indian teams building global products.

But the site itself has no geographic target. A student in Nairobi and a lawyer in Stockholm and a designer in São Paulo should all have the same experience. Free, fast, and private.

How we stay free

We don't charge for Desi Jugad, we don't have a pro tier, and we don't intend to. The site is funded by unobtrusive display advertising and occasional coffee from kind users. Because we have no servers processing files, our running costs are genuinely tiny — a few dollars a month to keep the domain and CDN alive.

We've promised ourselves we won't add dark patterns, we won't gate popular tools behind sign-ups, and we won't sell the site to someone who will. If we ever have to break those promises we'll explain why, loudly.

Open source, open libraries

Every conversion library we use is open source — the same code you can find on GitHub, used by major companies, reviewed by thousands of developers. We use heic2any, pdf.js, jsPDF, SheetJS, js-yaml, marked.js, and a handful of others. When you use Desi Jugad, you're using these tools — we just wrap them in an easy interface.

Feedback welcome

If there's a format pair we don't support and you think we should, let us know. If you hit a bug, we want to hear about it. If you built something on top of our tools, we'd love to see it.
