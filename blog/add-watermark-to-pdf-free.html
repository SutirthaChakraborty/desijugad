How to add a watermark to a PDF free online (2026)

6 min read·April 2026

Watermarks serve two purposes: protecting documents from unauthorised distribution, and communicating document status (DRAFT, CONFIDENTIAL, FOR REVIEW). Adding a watermark used to require Adobe Acrobat Pro or Microsoft Word. Today you can do it free in your browser — no account, no upload, no watermark on the watermark tool itself.

When you need to watermark a PDF

  • Legal documents: Mark contracts and agreements as DRAFT or FINAL before sharing with counterparties.
  • Confidential reports: Internal financial reports, HR documents, and board presentations shared externally need CONFIDENTIAL stamps.
  • Portfolio protection: Architects, photographers, and designers watermark sample work shared with prospective clients.
  • Proof of origin: Add your company name or website URL to documents before sharing as evidence they originate from you.
  • Educational materials: Mark assessment papers as SAMPLE to prevent misuse.

Types of PDF watermarks

Text watermarks

The most common type — diagonal text across each page. Can be:

  • Quick presets: CONFIDENTIAL, DRAFT, COPY, SAMPLE, FOR REVIEW, DO NOT COPY
  • Custom text: your company name, client name, project code
  • Dynamic: date/time, page number, recipient name

Image watermarks

Your logo or a stamp image placed on each page. Semi-transparent overlay that brands the document without obscuring the content significantly.

How to add a watermark to a PDF free (no upload)

  1. Open the PDF Watermark tool.
  2. Drop your PDF file.
  3. Choose watermark type: Text or Image.
  4. For text: enter your text or choose a preset (CONFIDENTIAL, DRAFT, etc.).
  5. Customise: font size, colour, opacity (30% default is good for most cases), rotation angle (default 45°).
  6. Choose which pages: all pages, or specific pages (e.g., first and last only).
  7. Click Add Watermark & Download.

Processed entirely in your browser using pdf-lib. Confidential documents stay on your device.

Best practices for professional watermarks

  • Opacity 20–35%: Visible enough to serve its purpose but not so dark it obscures the content. Opacity above 50% is usually excessive.
  • Diagonal placement (45°): Harder to crop or remove than horizontal placement.
  • Multiple instances: Tiling the watermark multiple times across the page (repeated pattern) is harder to remove in a graphics editor.
  • High-contrast colour: Grey watermarks on white paper may not reproduce well when printed in black and white. Red or dark grey are safer choices.
  • Font size: 60–80pt for A4 documents ensures the watermark is readable at normal viewing distance.

Can PDF watermarks be removed?

Text and image watermarks added as overlays to a PDF can potentially be removed by someone with Acrobat Pro or advanced PDF editing software. They are not tamper-proof. For truly confidential documents:

  • Add the watermark as a rasterized layer (convert to image PDF first, then watermark).
  • Use access controls and DRM if security is critical.
  • Watermarks are a deterrent and a legal paper trail, not a security mechanism.
