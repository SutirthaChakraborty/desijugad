PAN Card Number Validator — Check PAN Format Free Online

Published April 28, 2026 · 5 min read

Your PAN (Permanent Account Number) is one of the most important tax identifiers in India. But entering a PAN number in the wrong format when filing ITR, applying for a loan, or onboarding a vendor can cause immediate rejections. What is a PAN Card Number?

A PAN card is issued by the Income Tax Department of India (under the Ministry of Finance) through NSDL (Protean eGov Technologies) and UTIITSL. Every individual, company, HUF, or firm that pays income tax must have a unique PAN. It is also required for:

  • Opening a bank account
  • Applying for a credit card or loan
  • Buying or selling property above ₹5 lakh
  • Filing ITR (Income Tax Returns)
  • Cash transactions above ₹50,000
  • Foreign exchange transactions
  • GST registration

PAN Number Format — AAAAA9999A Explained

A valid PAN is always exactly 10 characters in the format: AAAAA9999A (5 letters + 4 digits + 1 letter), all uppercase.

Character-by-character breakdown

  • Characters 1–3: Any 3 uppercase letters (AAA to ZZZ) — alphabetic series assigned sequentially.
  • Character 4: Taxpayer type code:
    • P — Individual person
    • C — Company
    • H — Hindu Undivided Family (HUF)
    • F — Firm / Partnership
    • A — Association of Persons (AOP)
    • B — Body of Individuals (BOI)
    • G — Government entity
    • J — Artificial Juridical Person
    • L — Local authority
    • T — Trust
  • Character 5: First letter of the taxpayer's surname (for individuals) or name (for entities).
  • Characters 6–9: Unique running number (0001–9999).
  • Character 10: Single alphabetic check digit assigned by NSDL.

Example: ABCPK1234D — A-B-C = series, P = individual, K = surname starts with K, 1234 = unique number, D = check digit.

How to Validate a PAN Number (Format Check)

The simplest validation uses a regular expression: [A-Z]{5}[0-9]{4}[A-Z]{1}

This checks:

  1. Exactly 5 uppercase letters
  2. Exactly 4 digits
  3. Exactly 1 uppercase letter
  4. Total length of exactly 10 characters

You can also tighten validation by checking that character 4 is one of the valid taxpayer codes (P, C, H, F, A, B, G, J, L, T).

Common PAN Validation Errors

  • Lowercase letters: PAN must always be uppercase. "abcpk1234d" is invalid.
  • Wrong length: PAN is always exactly 10 characters. 9 or 11 chars = invalid.
  • Invalid 4th character: Only P, C, H, F, A, B, G, J, L, T are valid at position 4.
  • Special characters: No spaces, hyphens, or symbols allowed.
  • All zeros in digits section: 0000 is not a valid running number.

PAN vs Aadhaar vs TAN — Key Differences

  • PAN: 10-character alphanumeric. For income tax and financial transactions. Issued by Income Tax Department via NSDL/UTIITSL.
  • Aadhaar: 12-digit numeric. For identity. Issued by UIDAI. Can be linked with PAN for enhanced verification.
  • TAN: 10-character alphanumeric (like PAN but starts differently). Required for deducting/collecting TDS. Issued by NSDL.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I have two PAN cards?

No. Holding more than one PAN is illegal under Section 272B of the Income Tax Act and attracts a penalty of ₹10,000. If you have been issued two PANs by mistake, surrender the duplicate to the Income Tax Department.

What is e-PAN?

e-PAN is a digitally signed PDF version of your PAN card with a QR code. It is legally valid and can be downloaded for free from the NSDL or UTIITSL portal. It carries the same weight as a physical PAN card.

How do I check if a PAN number is real (not just correct format)?

Format validation only tells you if the PAN looks right structurally. To verify if a PAN is actually registered, use the Income Tax e-Filing portal or the Know Your PAN service. Note: this requires you to share the PAN number with the IT department's system.

Is linking PAN with Aadhaar mandatory?

Yes, as per the Income Tax Department guidelines, linking PAN with Aadhaar is mandatory for individuals. Failure to link by the deadline makes the PAN inoperative for filing ITR, TDS deductions, and financial transactions.
