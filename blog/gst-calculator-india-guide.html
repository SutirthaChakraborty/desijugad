GST calculator India 2026 — all slabs, CGST SGST IGST explained

10 min read·April 2026

GST (Goods and Services Tax) replaced over a dozen central and state taxes in India on 1 July 2017. It's now the primary indirect tax across the country. Whether you're a freelancer raising an invoice, a business owner buying materials, or a consumer trying to understand why a restaurant bill is higher than expected — knowing how to calculate GST is essential.

GST exclusive vs GST inclusive — the key difference

This is the most common source of confusion for small business owners and freelancers.

GST exclusive (more common for B2B)

The quoted price does not include GST. GST is added on top. Example:

  • Service charge: ₹10,000
  • GST @ 18%: ₹1,800
  • Total invoice: ₹11,800

Formula: GST = Original Price × (Rate/100)

GST inclusive (common for retail/B2C)

The displayed price already includes GST. You need to extract the original price and the tax component. Example:

  • MRP on product: ₹590
  • Original price (excl. GST @ 18%): ₹590 / 1.18 = ₹500
  • GST component: ₹590 − ₹500 = ₹90

Formula: Original = Total / (1 + Rate/100)

CGST vs SGST vs IGST — what's the difference?

This depends on whether the supply is intra-state or inter-state.

Intra-state supply (both seller and buyer in same state)

The GST is split equally between the Centre and the State:

  • CGST (Central GST) = GST Rate / 2
  • SGST (State GST) = GST Rate / 2

Example: 18% GST on a service in Maharashtra → 9% CGST + 9% SGST

Inter-state supply (buyer and seller in different states)

  • IGST (Integrated GST) = Full GST Rate

Example: A company in Delhi invoicing a client in Bengaluru → 18% IGST (not split)

The Centre distributes IGST to the destination state as SGST — the consumer never sees this mechanism.

Who needs to register for GST?

As of 2026, GST registration is mandatory if:

  • Your annual turnover exceeds ₹40 lakh (goods) or ₹20 lakh (services) for most states.
  • For special category states (Jammu & Kashmir, Uttarakhand, Himachal, etc.), the threshold is ₹10 lakh.
  • You sell goods on e-commerce platforms (Amazon, Flipkart) — registration required regardless of turnover.
  • You make inter-state supplies — registration required regardless of turnover.
