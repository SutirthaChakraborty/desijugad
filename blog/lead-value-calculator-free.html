Lead Value Calculator — Know Your Marketing ROI

Published April 27, 2026

The Problem: Flying Blind with Marketing Spend

Many businesses spend on ads without knowing if they're profitable. "We spent ₹50,000 on Google Ads and got 200 leads" sounds great, but what if only 5 became customers? At ₹250 per lead cost, if each customer is worth ₹1,000, the campaign loses money. You can't improve what you don't measure.

Essential Marketing Metrics

1. Cost Per Lead (CPL)

CPL = Total Ad Spend ÷ Number of Leads Generated

Example: ₹50,000 ad spend ÷ 200 leads = ₹250 per lead

2. Lead-to-Customer Conversion Rate

What % of leads actually buy?

  • B2B typical: 2-5%
  • E-commerce typical: 1-3%
  • SaaS typical: 5-10%

3. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

CAC = Total Marketing Spend ÷ Number of Customers Acquired

Example: ₹50,000 ÷ (200 × 3%) = ₹50,000 ÷ 6 = ₹8,333 per customer

4. Lifetime Value (LTV)

LTV = Average Order Value × Purchase Frequency × Customer Lifespan

Example: ₹5,000 avg order × 4 purchases/year × 3 years = ₹60,000 LTV

The Rule: LTV Should Be 3x CAC

Healthy business: LTV = 3 × CAC or higher

In our example: ₹60,000 LTV ÷ ₹8,333 CAC = 7.2:1 ratio → Very profitable campaign!

If CAC > LTV, you're losing money on every customer (unsustainable).

How to Improve Your Lead Numbers

Lower CPL:

  • Improve ad copy targeting
  • Use lookalike audiences
  • Optimize landing page conversion
  • Test different ad platforms

Increase Conversion Rate:

  • Better lead qualification
  • Faster follow-up (within 1 hour)
  • Personalized sales outreach
  • Trust signals (reviews, testimonials)

Increase LTV:

  • Cross-sell and upsell
  • Improve customer retention
  • Increase product value
  • Build customer loyalty

Conclusion

Stop throwing money at marketing without measurement. Track your CPL, conversion rate, and LTV. Use a calculator to spot which campaigns work and which drain profits. This data drives better decisions and higher ROI.
