Barcode scanner online free — decode any barcode from image (2026)

7 min read·April 2026

Barcodes are on every product, package, and document — but reading them from images or screenshots is surprisingly non-trivial without the right tool. Our browser-based barcode scanner decodes virtually every barcode type from any image file, your webcam, or a pasted screenshot — instantly, free, and without any app installation.

Barcode types the scanner supports

1D barcodes (linear)

  • EAN-13: Standard retail barcode on most consumer products globally. 13 digits.
  • EAN-8: Compact EAN for small products. 8 digits.
  • UPC-A: North American retail standard. 12 digits.
  • UPC-E: Compact UPC for small packages.
  • Code 128: High-density, alphanumeric — used in shipping, logistics, and manufacturing.
  • Code 39: Older alphanumeric barcode. Common in industrial, automotive, and defence.
  • ITF (Interleaved 2 of 5): Used in shipping cartons and warehouse contexts.
  • Codabar: Used in blood banks, libraries, and FedEx airbills.

2D barcodes

  • QR Code: Ubiquitous — used for URLs, UPI payments, contact cards, WiFi.
  • Data Matrix: Used in pharmaceutical packaging, electronics components, and postal services.
  • PDF417: Used on Indian driving licences, ID cards, and airline boarding passes.
  • Aztec Code: Used on train tickets and transit passes.

Common use cases in India

  • Product verification: Scan the barcode on a product to get the EAN number, then check if the product is legitimate vs counterfeit.
  • Driving licence: The 2D barcode on Indian driving licences (PDF417) encodes all your licence data — scan it to verify the encoded data matches printed data.
  • Parcel tracking: Courier barcodes (Code 128) can be decoded to get the tracking number when the printed number is hard to read.
  • UPI QR codes: Decode to verify the UPI ID and merchant name before making payment.
  • Inventory management: Businesses without a barcode scanner can decode product barcodes via the web tool.
  • Developer testing: Verify that barcodes generated by your application decode correctly.

How to use the barcode scanner

  1. Open the Barcode Scanner.
  2. Choose input: upload image, use webcam, or paste screenshot (Ctrl+V).
  3. The scanner detects the barcode in the image automatically — even if the barcode is part of a larger image.
  4. Result shows: barcode type, decoded value, and for EAN codes, the country of origin.
  5. Copy the decoded value to clipboard with one click.

Why barcodes fail to scan

If the scanner can't read a barcode, common causes:

  • Blur: The image is too blurry. Barcodes need sharp edges to decode. Take a higher-resolution photo.
  • Angle: Perspective distortion from scanning at an angle. Scan straight-on.
  • Low contrast: Faded, washed-out, or damaged barcode. Increase contrast in an image editor before scanning.
  • Partial barcode: The full barcode must be in the image. EAN-13 requires all 13 digit stripes to be present.
  • Reflection: Glossy packaging can cause glare that breaks the barcode pattern.
