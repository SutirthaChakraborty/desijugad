Pakistan Income Tax Calculator 2026 — Salary Tax FBR Guide

Published April 28, 2026 · 7 min read

Income tax in Pakistan is administered by the Federal Board of Revenue (FBR). For salaried individuals, tax is deducted at source (withholding tax) each month by your employer. This guide explains Pakistan's tax slabs for FY 2025-26 and how to calculate your net take-home salary.

Pakistan Income Tax Slabs FY 2025-26 (Salaried Persons)

  • 0% — Up to PKR 600,000 annual income
  • 5% — PKR 600,001 to PKR 1,200,000
  • 15% — PKR 1,200,001 to PKR 2,200,000
  • 25% — PKR 2,200,001 to PKR 3,200,000
  • 30% — PKR 3,200,001 to PKR 4,100,000
  • 35% — Above PKR 4,100,000

Note: Non-filers (those who do not file income tax returns) pay higher withholding tax rates. Always file your return to avoid surcharges.

Other Deductions from Pakistan Salary

EOBI (Employees' Old-Age Benefits Institution)

  • Employee contribution: 1% of monthly wages
  • Employer contribution: 5% of minimum wage
  • Maximum monthly wage for EOBI: PKR 20,000 (ceiling)

Provincial Social Security (SESSI/PESSI/SESSI-KP)

  • Sindh (SESSI): Employee 1%, Employer 6%
  • Punjab (PESSI): Employee 1%, Employer 6%
  • Applies to workers earning up to the provincial wage ceiling

Pakistan's Sales Tax (GST)

Pakistan's sales tax rate is 17% (federal). Unlike India's GST, Pakistan's sales tax does not have multiple slabs — a single 17% rate applies to most goods. Additional provincial sales taxes apply to services.

Example Calculation (Pakistan)

Annual salary: PKR 2,400,000

  • First PKR 600,000: 0% = PKR 0
  • Next PKR 600,000 (600K-1.2M): 5% = PKR 30,000
  • Next PKR 1,000,000 (1.2M-2.2M): 15% = PKR 150,000
  • Remaining PKR 200,000 (2.2M-2.4M): 25% = PKR 50,000
  • Total tax = PKR 230,000/year = PKR 19,167/month
  • EOBI ≈ PKR 200/month
  • Net monthly = PKR 2,400,000/12 - PKR 19,167 - PKR 200 ≈ PKR 180,600/month

How to File Income Tax Return in Pakistan

  1. Register on FBR's IRIS portal (iris.fbr.gov.pk)
  2. Obtain your NTN (National Tax Number)
  3. File Form-I (salary income declaration) by September 30 each year
  4. Submit e-payment if tax is due

Salaried employees whose entire income is from one employer and tax is already deducted at source may be exempt from filing, but filing is recommended to become a filer and access lower withholding tax rates.

Pakistan vs India Tax Comparison

  • India's new tax regime: 0% up to Rs 12L (with rebate); max rate 30%
  • Pakistan: 0% up to PKR 600K; max rate 35%
  • Both countries use progressive tax brackets
  • Pakistan has fewer deduction options than India's old regime

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between filer and non-filer in Pakistan?

A filer is someone registered on the Active Taxpayers List (ATL) who files annual income tax returns. Non-filers pay double or triple withholding tax rates on bank transactions, property purchases, vehicle registration, and more. Always file your return to become a filer.

Is foreign income taxable in Pakistan?

Pakistani residents are taxed on worldwide income. However, remittances from abroad sent through banking channels have special provisions — consult an FBR-registered tax consultant for details.
