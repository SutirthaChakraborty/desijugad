Pakistan Income Tax Calculator 2026 — Salary Tax FBR Guide

Published April 28, 2026 · 7 min read

Pakistan's Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) administers income tax under the Income Tax Ordinance 2001. For tax year 2025-2026 (income earned from July 1, 2025 to June 30, 2026), significant changes were introduced in the Finance Act 2024, including revised salary tax slabs and new withholding provisions. This guide covers what salaried individuals and business owners need to know to calculate and plan their tax liability accurately.

Filer vs. Non-Filer: The Critical Distinction

Pakistan has a unique "filer vs. non-filer" system that affects tax rates on many transactions:

  • Active taxpayer (filer): Filed an income tax return for the latest tax year. Eligible for lower withholding tax rates on property purchases, vehicle registration, banking transactions, and dividends.
  • Non-filer: Not on the Active Taxpayer List (ATL). Subject to double or higher withholding tax rates on many transactions.

For example, withholding tax on property purchase for a filer is 1% while a non-filer pays 2%. On a Rs. 2 crore property, that's a Rs. 2 lakh difference. Filing a return — even with no tax due — is financially beneficial for most Pakistanis.

Key Deductions and Exemptions for Salaried Employees

  • Zakat deducted at source: Fully deductible from taxable income
  • Workers' Welfare Fund (WWF): 2% of income above Rs. 500,000 — deductible
  • Profit on debt (house loan interest): Up to Rs. 2 million deductible for housing finance
  • Education expenses: Tuition fees paid for children at Pakistani educational institutions (specified limits)
  • Life insurance premiums: Premiums paid to approved insurers are deductible
  • Medical allowance: Up to 10% of basic salary is exempt from tax if received as medical allowance
  • House Rent Allowance: 45% of basic salary or Rs. 720,000 per year, whichever is lower, is exempt

Business Income vs. Salary: Key Differences

Salaried income and business income are taxed differently in Pakistan:

  • Salaried income: Taxed at progressive rates shown above. Employer deducts tax at source (withholding). Employee files annual return to reconcile.
  • Business income: Subject to the same progressive rates, but with different rules for allowable deductions (cost of goods sold, operating expenses, depreciation). Business owners must maintain proper books of accounts.
  • Rental income: Taxed separately at progressive rates. 20% deduction allowed for repairs, maintenance, and insurance without proof.
  • Capital gains: Different rates apply depending on holding period. Immovable property: 15% if held over 4 years (reducing progressively for longer holdings). Securities: 12.5% for open-end mutual funds, 15% for others.

Return Filing Deadlines

Pakistan's income tax year runs from July 1 to June 30. Key dates:

  • Individual salaried persons: September 30 of the relevant year
  • Business individuals (AOP, companies): December 31
  • Extension requests: Can be filed with Commissioner IR before the due date

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my monthly take-home salary after tax in Pakistan?

Calculate your annual taxable salary (annual gross minus exempt allowances), apply the slab tax, divide by 12 for monthly tax deduction. For example: annual taxable income of Rs. 18 lakh falls in the 3rd slab — tax is Rs. 30,000 + 15% of Rs. 6 lakh = Rs. 1,20,000. Monthly tax deduction: Rs. 10,000. What is the deadline for filing income tax returns in Pakistan?

For salaried individuals, the deadline is September 30. For businesses and associations of persons (AOP), it's December 31. Missing the deadline results in a default surcharge of 0.1% of tax payable per day, or Rs. 500 per day (whichever is higher), up to 50% of tax payable.

Do overseas Pakistanis need to file a tax return?

Pakistani nationals living abroad are generally considered non-resident for tax purposes if they spend fewer than 183 days in Pakistan during the tax year. Non-residents are taxed only on Pakistan-source income. However, if you have property, bank accounts, or business interests in Pakistan, you may still have filing obligations. Overseas Pakistanis should consult a tax advisor for their specific situation.
