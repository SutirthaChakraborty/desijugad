Retirement Calculator — Plan Your Retirement Savings in India

Published April 27, 2026

How Much Do You Need for Retirement?

Most financial advisors suggest you'll need 70-80% of your pre-retirement income to maintain your lifestyle after retirement. But with increasing life expectancy (85+ years), inflation (6% annually), and changing expenses, calculating the exact amount is complex. A retirement calculator does this automatically.

Key Variables in Retirement Planning

1. Current Age & Retirement Age

The earlier you start, the less you need to save monthly. Retiring at 55 vs 60 requires 28% more corpus.

2. Life Expectancy

Indian life expectancy is ~71 years, but many live to 85+. Plan for 90-95 for safety.

3. Monthly Expenses

Current lifestyle cost is the baseline. Adjust for post-retirement changes (no commute, lower taxes, healthcare increases).

4. Inflation

At 6% inflation, ₹50,000 monthly today becomes ₹74,000 in 10 years. Calculators adjust for this automatically.

5. Investment Returns

Conservative estimate: 8% for balanced portfolio. This compounds your savings over decades.

Retirement Savings Strategies

1. NPS (National Pension Scheme)

Government-backed, tax benefits up to ₹2.5 lakh/year. Good returns (7-9%) with flexibility.

2. PPF (Public Provident Fund)

Safe 7.1% returns, 15-year lockup, tax-free withdrawals. Excellent for steady savers.

3. Mutual Funds (SIPs)

Higher returns (10-12%) for long-term investors. Flexible contributions and withdrawal.

4. Real Estate

Tangible asset, rental income potential, but illiquid and maintenance-heavy.

The 4% Withdrawal Rule

A safe retirement strategy: withdraw 4% of your corpus annually. For a ₹1 crore corpus, that's ₹4 lakh/year. This money typically lasts 30+ years without running out.

  2. Calculate monthly savings needed to reach that goal
  3. Choose appropriate investment vehicles (NPS, PPF, Mutual Funds)
  4. Review your plan annually and adjust
  5. Start as early as possible — time is your biggest asset

Conclusion

Retirement planning isn't complicated once you know your target number. Use a calculator to set your goal, create a plan, and invest consistently. Your future self will thank you!
