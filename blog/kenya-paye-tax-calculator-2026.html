Kenya PAYE Tax Calculator 2026 — Salary Deductions & Net Pay Guide

Published April 28, 2026 · 8 min read

Pay As You Earn (PAYE) is Kenya's system for collecting income tax from employment income. Administered by the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA), PAYE is deducted by your employer from your monthly salary and remitted to KRA by the 9th of the following month. Understanding how PAYE is calculated helps you verify your payslip, plan your finances, and ensure you're claiming all available reliefs.

Kenya PAYE Tax Bands for 2025-2026

Following changes in the Finance Act 2023, Kenya's PAYE structure is:

Monthly Taxable Income (KES)Tax Rate
First KES 24,00010%
Next KES 8,333 (KES 24,001 – 32,333)25%
Next KES 467,667 (KES 32,334 – 500,000)30%
Next KES 300,000 (KES 500,001 – 800,000)32.5%
Above KES 800,00035%

These are monthly rates applied to monthly taxable income. Annual rates apply to annual income by simply multiplying the monthly thresholds by 12.

Personal Relief: Kenya's Basic Tax Reduction

Every Kenyan resident taxpayer is entitled to a Personal Relief of KES 2,400 per month (KES 28,800 per year). This is deducted directly from the calculated tax — not from income. It effectively means the first KES 24,000 of monthly income is tax-free (10% tax on KES 24,000 = KES 2,400 = exactly the personal relief amount).

In practice: if your calculated gross PAYE is KES 5,000, you subtract KES 2,400 personal relief to get a net PAYE of KES 2,600.

Other Mandatory Deductions That Reduce Taxable Income

Before calculating PAYE, several mandatory deductions reduce your taxable income:

  • NSSF (National Social Security Fund): From February 2023, contributions are based on a tiered system. Tier I: 6% of pensionable pay up to KES 6,000 (max KES 360 employee + KES 360 employer). Tier II: 6% of pensionable pay between KES 6,001 and KES 18,000 (max KES 720). Employee contribution is deductible from taxable income.
  • NHIF / SHA (Social Health Authority): From October 2024, NHIF was replaced by SHA (Social Health Authority). SHA contributions are income-based: 2.75% of gross salary (capped). Deductible from taxable income.
  • Registered pension contributions: Contributions to a registered pension scheme are deductible up to KES 20,000 per month (KES 240,000 per year).
  • Mortgage interest relief: Interest on housing loans up to KES 25,000 per month is deductible.
  • Life insurance premiums: Premiums paid to approved insurers, up to KES 5,000 per month.

Step-by-Step PAYE Calculation Example

For an employee earning KES 80,000 gross salary monthly:

  1. Gross salary: KES 80,000
  2. Less NSSF (employee): approximately KES 1,080
  3. Less SHA contribution (2.75%): KES 2,200
  4. Taxable income: KES 80,000 – KES 1,080 – KES 2,200 = KES 76,720
  5. PAYE on KES 76,720:
    • First KES 24,000 × 10% = KES 2,400
    • Next KES 8,333 × 25% = KES 2,083
    • Remaining KES 44,387 × 30% = KES 13,316
    • Total gross PAYE = KES 17,799
  6. Less personal relief: KES 2,400
  7. Net PAYE payable: KES 15,399
  8. Take-home: KES 80,000 – KES 1,080 – KES 2,200 – KES 15,399 = approximately KES 61,321

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the PAYE-free monthly income threshold in Kenya?

Due to personal relief of KES 2,400 per month, income up to KES 24,000 per month effectively attracts zero net PAYE. 10% tax on KES 24,000 is exactly KES 2,400, which is fully offset by the personal relief. Income above this level attracts progressive PAYE.

How do I file a KRA PAYE return?

Employers are responsible for filing monthly PAYE returns through the KRA iTax portal by the 9th of the following month. Employees file individual annual income tax returns by June 30 if they have income from multiple sources, other income besides employment, or need to claim additional reliefs. Most employees with a single employer don't need to file annual returns separately.

Does PAYE apply to casual workers and part-time employees?

Yes. PAYE applies to all employment income, including casual and part-time work. The same tax bands and personal relief apply. However, casual employees paid daily rates below a threshold may be subject to different withholding procedures — confirm with your employer or KRA for your specific arrangement.

Disclaimer: The PAYE bands and relief figures on this page are for informational purposes only. Kenya Revenue Authority updates tax rules annually via the Finance Act. Always verify current figures with KRA at kra.go.ke or consult a certified public accountant for your specific situation.
