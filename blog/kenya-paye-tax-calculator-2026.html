Kenya PAYE Tax Calculator 2026 — Salary Deductions & Net Pay Guide

Published April 28, 2026 · 8 min read

Every salaried employee in Kenya has PAYE (Pay As You Earn) tax and statutory deductions taken from their salary every month. Understanding these deductions helps you verify your payslip and plan your finances. This guide covers Kenya's tax bands, NHIF, NSSF, and the Housing Levy.

Kenya PAYE Tax Bands 2026

  • 10% — Monthly income KSh 0 – KSh 24,000
  • 25% — Monthly income KSh 24,001 – KSh 32,333
  • 30% — Monthly income above KSh 32,333

Personal relief: KSh 2,400/month (reduces your monthly tax by this amount).

Insurance relief: 15% of insurance premiums paid (for life, health insurance), capped at KSh 5,000/month.

NHIF (National Health Insurance Fund)

NHIF contributions by salary band (monthly):

  • KSh 0 – 5,999: KSh 150
  • KSh 6,000 – 7,999: KSh 300
  • KSh 8,000 – 11,999: KSh 400
  • KSh 12,000 – 14,999: KSh 500
  • KSh 15,000 – 19,999: KSh 600
  • KSh 20,000 – 24,999: KSh 750
  • KSh 25,000 – 29,999: KSh 850
  • KSh 30,000 – 34,999: KSh 900
  • KSh 35,000 – 39,999: KSh 950
  • KSh 40,000 – 44,999: KSh 1,000
  • KSh 45,000 – 49,999: KSh 1,100
  • KSh 50,000 – 59,999: KSh 1,200
  • KSh 60,000 – 69,999: KSh 1,300
  • KSh 70,000 – 79,999: KSh 1,400
  • KSh 80,000 – 89,999: KSh 1,500
  • KSh 90,000 – 99,999: KSh 1,600
  • KSh 100,000+: KSh 1,700

NSSF (National Social Security Fund) — New Rates

Under the NSSF Act 2013 (implementation phased):

  • Tier I: 6% employee + 6% employer on first KSh 6,000 of pensionable pay
  • Tier II: 6% employee + 6% employer on pensionable pay above KSh 6,000 up to Upper Earnings Limit (subject to annual review)
  • Maximum employee contribution: capped per current upper earnings limit

Housing Levy (Affordable Housing)

Introduced in Finance Act 2023:

  • Rate: 1.5% of gross monthly salary (employee) + 1.5% employer contribution
  • No upper cap on the levy
  • Can be used for mortgage down payment when buying an affordable housing unit

Example Calculation (Kenya)

Monthly gross salary: KSh 80,000

  • PAYE: (24,000×10% + 8,333×25% + 47,667×30%) - KSh 2,400 personal relief = KSh 14,133 - KSh 2,400 = KSh 11,733
  • NHIF: KSh 1,500
  • NSSF: ~KSh 2,160 (Tier I + Tier II combined estimate)
  • Housing Levy (1.5%): KSh 1,200
  • Total deductions: ~KSh 16,593
  • Net take-home: ~KSh 63,407/month

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I check if my employer is remitting PAYE correctly?

Log into KRA's iTax portal (itax.kra.go.ke) with your KRA PIN. Under PAYE, you can see tax remittances made against your PIN. Mismatches should be reported to KRA.

What is a P9 form in Kenya?

P9A is a tax deduction card (certificate) issued by your employer showing total gross pay and PAYE deducted during the year. You need it to file your annual Individual Income Tax Return on iTax.

Do I need to file a tax return if all my income is from salary?

Yes. All taxpayers in Kenya must file a return annually, even if only employment income and all tax was withheld. File a nil return or employment income return on iTax by June 30.
