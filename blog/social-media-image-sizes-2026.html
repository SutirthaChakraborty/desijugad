Social media image sizes 2026: the complete cheat sheet

10 min read · Updated April 2026

Social platforms change their image specifications silently. A Twitter/X card that looked perfect last year now gets cropped differently. LinkedIn changed its cover photo ratio again. YouTube updated its thumbnail preview zone. This guide reflects the current specifications as of April 2026 — bookmark it and check back, because these change regularly.

The safe zone rule

For cover photos and banners (Facebook cover, YouTube channel art, LinkedIn background), every platform displays the image differently across devices. The safe zone is the area that's always visible on both desktop and mobile — keep logos, names, and important visuals inside it.

  • Facebook cover (820×312): Safe zone is the central 640×312
  • YouTube banner (2560×1440): Safe zone is the central 1546×423
  • LinkedIn background (1584×396): Safe zone is the central 1192×220 (approximately)
  • Twitter/X header (1500×500): Safe zone is the central 1500×333
