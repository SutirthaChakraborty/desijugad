How to make AI social media posts free — complete guide 2026

8 min read · Published April 2026

You want to post consistently but you're running out of ideas, time, or both. AI can solve the ideas-and-time part — but most AI social media tools cost $20–$100/month. This guide covers how to use free AI tools to generate captions, hashtags, and platform-sized images for every major social network, and how to build a workflow that actually saves time rather than creating new busywork.

What AI can (and can't) do for social media

AI is genuinely useful for:

  • Caption drafting — give it a topic, brand tone, and platform, and it produces a working first draft in seconds
  • Hashtag research — generating relevant hashtags based on content and niche
  • Image generation — creating original images from text descriptions (product mockups, illustrations, backgrounds)
  • Content repurposing — adapting a LinkedIn post to Instagram format, or a Twitter thread to a carousel
  • Content calendar ideas — listing 30 post ideas for a given niche in seconds

AI is not good at:

  • Brand authenticity — generic AI captions sound generic; they need editing to sound like you
  • Current events — training data has a cutoff; don't ask AI about things that happened recently without verifying
  • Niche-specific jargon and community references — these need to come from you
  • Strategy — AI can create content ideas but can't tell you which content will perform best for your specific audience

How to generate a social media post with AI — step by step

Method 1: Use AI Social Post Maker (all-in-one)

  3. Select your target platform (Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Facebook, Pinterest).

Method 2: ChatGPT + Photo Editor (manual control)

If you prefer manual control or want to use your own photo instead of an AI-generated image:

  1. Open ChatGPT (free tier works). Paste this prompt template:
    Write a [PLATFORM] [TONE] caption for: [YOUR TOPIC]. Include [NUMBER] relevant hashtags. Max [CHARACTER LIMIT] characters total. My audience is [DESCRIPTION OF AUDIENCE].
  2. Copy and edit the output caption.
  3. Open Desi Jugad Photo Editor, load your image, and use the platform preset to resize it to the correct dimensions for your target network.
  4. Download and post.

Prompt formulas that actually work

Most AI social media output is generic because the prompts are generic. Here are prompt formulas that produce better results:

For Instagram (product/brand)

Write an Instagram caption for [BRAND TYPE] selling [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Tone: [CASUAL/PROFESSIONAL/WITTY]. Hook in first line (no emoji). Body: 2–3 sentences about the benefit, not the feature. CTA: [DESIRED ACTION]. 5–7 hashtags, mix of niche and broad. Under 300 characters before hashtags.

For LinkedIn (thought leadership)

Write a LinkedIn post from the perspective of [ROLE/INDUSTRY]. Insight: [COUNTERINTUITIVE OBSERVATION about your niche]. Format: opening hook, 3–5 bullet points or short paragraphs, actionable takeaway. Professional but conversational. No hashtag spam — max 3 hashtags at the end. Under 1300 characters.

For Twitter/X

Write 3 tweet options about [TOPIC]. Style: [EDUCATIONAL/OPINIONATED/QUESTION]. Each under 240 characters. No hashtags (they reduce reach on X). Strong opening word. Conversational language.

Hashtag strategy by platform

  • Instagram: 5–10 hashtags. Mix of sizes: 1–2 large (1M+ posts), 3–5 medium (100K–1M), 2–3 small/niche (<100K). Avoid banned hashtags — search them first.
  • Twitter/X: 0–2 hashtags. Research by X's own team found posts with hashtags perform worse than those without. Use them only for events or trending topics.
  • LinkedIn: 3–5 hashtags. Stick to professional industry terms. Put them at the end of the post.
  • Facebook: 0–3 hashtags. Low impact on organic reach; Facebook's own data suggests they don't significantly boost reach.
  • Pinterest: Not applicable — Pinterest uses keyword-based search, not hashtag-based. Put keywords in the pin title and description instead.

Building a 30-post content calendar with AI in under an hour

Use this prompt to generate a month's worth of post ideas at once:

I run a [TYPE OF BUSINESS/ACCOUNT] on [PLATFORM]. My audience is [DESCRIPTION]. Give me 30 social media post ideas for the next month. Mix the following content types evenly: educational (teaching something), entertaining (relatable or funny), promotional (product/service), and inspirational. Format as a numbered list with the content type labelled.

Then turn each idea into a full post using the platform-specific prompts above. One hour of AI-assisted work can produce a month of content drafts that you refine and schedule.
