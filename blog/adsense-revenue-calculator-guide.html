AdSense revenue calculator: how much can your blog earn? (2026)

9 min read·April 2026

One of the first questions every blogger asks is "how much will I make from AdSense?" The answer depends on three variables: page views, click-through rate (CTR), and cost per click (CPC). Understanding these — and the benchmarks for your niche and traffic geography — lets you set realistic earning expectations and identify the most impactful levers to pull.

AdSense revenue formula

The basic formula:

Revenue = (Page Views × CTR / 100) × CPC

Or equivalently:

Revenue = Page Views × (RPM / 1000)

Where RPM (Revenue Per Mille) = earnings per 1,000 page views = CTR% × CPC × 10.

Key terms explained

  • Page Views: Total pages loaded per month (not unique visitors).
  • CTR (Click-Through Rate): % of page views where a visitor clicks an ad. Typical range: 0.5%–3%. Average is ~1%.
  • CPC (Cost Per Click): What advertisers pay Google per click. You receive ~68% of this. Ranges from ₹1 to ₹500+ depending on niche and geography.
  • RPM: Earnings per 1,000 page views. The most useful metric for comparing performance.
  • CPM: Cost per 1,000 impressions (from the advertiser side). For display ads that pay per impression (not click).

Why India traffic pays less than US/UK traffic

Advertisers set bids based on the value of a customer in each geography. A click from a US visitor on a loan ad is worth more because US loans are higher value. A click from an Indian visitor on the same ad is worth less in absolute dollar terms — though the gap has narrowed significantly in recent years as Indian digital ad spend has grown.

Strategies to increase effective RPM from India traffic:

  • Target high-CPC niches (finance, legal, B2B SaaS)
  • Write English content that also attracts US/UK search traffic
  • Use auto ads with anchor and interstitial formats for higher fill rates
  • Improve page speed — slow pages increase bounce rate, reducing ad impressions per session
