Bangladesh Income Tax Calculator 2026 — Salary Tax NBR Guide

Published April 28, 2026 · 7 min read

Bangladesh's income tax system was significantly reformed under the Income Tax Act 2023, which replaced the decades-old Income Tax Ordinance 1984. For the 2025-2026 assessment year (income earned in the 2024-2025 fiscal year), Bangladeshi taxpayers need to understand the new structure, exemption limits, and return filing requirements. This guide breaks down everything you need to calculate your tax accurately.

Bangladesh Income Tax Slabs for 2025-2026

For individual taxpayers (other than women, senior citizens, and people with disabilities), the tax slabs are:

Income Slab (BDT)Tax Rate
Up to ৳3,50,000Nil (tax-free)

৳4,50,001 – ৳7,50,00010%

Special exemptions: Women and senior citizens (65+ years) get a higher basic exemption of ৳4,00,000. Persons with disabilities get ৳4,75,000. Third gender individuals get ৳4,75,000. Freedom fighters get ৳5,00,000.

Minimum Tax (Even If Calculation Shows Zero)

A key feature of Bangladesh's tax system is the minimum tax requirement. Even if your calculated tax is zero (because your income is below the taxable threshold), you must still pay a minimum tax if you file a return. The minimum tax depends on your location:

  • Dhaka and Chittagong City Corporation areas: ৳5,000
  • Other City Corporation areas: ৳4,000
  • District headquarters areas: ৳3,000
  • All other areas: ৳2,000

This minimum tax applies to individuals who are required to file a return (TIN holders with specified financial activities) regardless of their taxable income.

Investment Tax Rebate: How to Reduce Your Tax

Bangladesh offers a significant tax rebate for eligible investments and expenses. You can claim a 15% rebate on allowable investments, up to a maximum of the lower of:

  • 3% of total income
  • ৳10,00,000 (10 lakh)
  • The actual investment made

Eligible investments include contributions to provident fund, life insurance premium, investment in DPS (Deposit Pension Scheme), purchase of savings certificates (Sanchayapatra), investment in shares or mutual funds listed on the stock exchange, and house rent for self-occupied property (up to specified limits).

Sources of Income and Tax Treatment

Bangladesh taxes income from seven heads:

  1. Salaries: Basic salary, allowances, bonuses. Festival allowances up to two months' basic salary are tax-exempt. House Rent Allowance up to ৳25,000/month or 50% of basic salary is exempt.
  2. Interest on securities: Government securities, bank fixed deposits.
  3. Income from house property: Rent received minus 30% standard deduction for repairs and maintenance.
  4. Agricultural income: Generally exempt for small farmers; taxable above ৳2,00,000 for larger operations.
  5. Income from business or profession: Net profit after deducting allowable business expenses.
  6. Capital gains: From sale of property, shares, etc. Different rates apply for short-term and long-term gains.
  7. Income from other sources: Dividends, royalties, lottery winnings.

Return Filing Deadlines and Requirements

The income tax return filing deadline in Bangladesh is 30 November for individual taxpayers. For company assessees, it's 15 January of the following year. You must have a Tax Identification Number (TIN/e-TIN) to file.

Filing is mandatory if you: earn above the basic exemption limit; own a motor vehicle; have a credit card; operate a bank account with total deposits exceeding ৳10 lakh in a year; hold a TIN; or engage in specified professions or business activities.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my Bangladesh income tax for 2025-2026?

Add all taxable income from different sources, subtract eligible deductions, apply the slab rates to the taxable income, then subtract the investment rebate (15% of eligible investments). The result is your payable tax, subject to the minimum tax in your city corporation area. Use the Desi Jugad Income Tax Calculator for instant calculation.

What happens if I don't file an income tax return?

Failure to file when required attracts a penalty of 10% of the last assessed tax or ৳1,000, whichever is higher. Interest at 2% per month also accrues on unpaid tax. Additionally, many government services (trade license renewal, bank loans, property registration) require proof of a filed return.

Is income from remittances taxable in Bangladesh?

Remittances sent by Bangladeshi nationals working abroad are generally exempt from income tax in Bangladesh. However, income earned in Bangladesh by the remitter when they return, or interest earned on remittance-derived savings, is taxable.
