Convertio vs CloudConvert vs Smallpdf vs Desi Jugad — the honest 2026 comparison

14 min read · Updated April 2026

Search "convert PDF to JPG" or "HEIC to JPG online" and the same names come up every time: Convertio, CloudConvert, Smallpdf, ILovePDF, and Adobe Express. They're all legitimate tools that work. But they make very different decisions about your files, your privacy, and your wallet — and those decisions matter more than most comparison articles bother to explain.

This review is written by the team behind Desi Jugad, a browser-based converter. We have an obvious interest in being chosen, so we'll be transparent: we'll tell you exactly when the other tools are better than us, and exactly where our approach wins. No affiliate links, no sponsored rankings.

The fundamental split: upload-based vs. browser-based

Every online converter falls into one of two categories:

Upload-based converters (Convertio, CloudConvert, Smallpdf, ILovePDF, Adobe Express, Zamzar, FreeConvert) transfer your file from your device to their server, run the conversion in their infrastructure, then send the result back. This is how most converters work, and it's a perfectly valid approach — the conversion engine runs on a powerful server and handles formats that are difficult to implement in a browser.

Browser-based converters (Desi Jugad, Squoosh by Google, some tools on PDF24) run the conversion logic directly inside your browser tab using JavaScript and WebAssembly. Your file never leaves your device. The tradeoff is a smaller format library — not everything that runs on a server can be compiled to WebAssembly yet.

Which approach is better depends entirely on your use case. Here's the breakdown for each major tool.

Convertio — best format library, server-side

Convertio supports over 300 format conversions and is among the most comprehensive tools available. It handles obscure format pairs that no browser-based tool supports: CAD files, 3D models, old Office formats, specialist audio formats. If you need to convert a .DWG to PDF or an .OGG to FLAC, Convertio probably handles it.

Free tier: 100 MB per file, 2 files at a time, no daily conversion limit. Files are deleted from their servers after 24 hours.

Privacy: Your files are uploaded to Convertio's servers. They use HTTPS and have a privacy policy committing to deletion after 24 hours. For most content this is fine. For medical records, legal documents, unreleased creative work, or anything confidential, understand that the file does exist on someone else's infrastructure for up to a day.

Pricing: Paid plans start at around $9.99/month for larger file sizes, longer retention, and higher concurrency.

Best for: Obscure format conversions, CAD files, batch processing of non-sensitive files, scenarios where browser support is weak.

CloudConvert — best quality output, most transparent

CloudConvert is probably the most developer-friendly and technically transparent of the upload-based converters. It has an excellent API, detailed documentation, and is widely used by businesses and developers to power conversion pipelines. The quality of output — particularly for PDFs and Office formats — is consistently high.

Free tier: 25 free conversion minutes per day. Most simple conversions take under a second of compute time, so you can do a reasonable number for free. But video conversions, large PDFs, or OCR tasks eat minutes quickly.

Privacy: CloudConvert's servers process your files. They're ISO 27001 certified and have a clear privacy policy. Files are deleted after processing (or after a retention window you configure via API). For a business handling sensitive documents, CloudConvert is the most auditable of the server-based options.

Pricing: Pay-as-you-go from around $8 for 500 conversion minutes, or subscription plans. For heavy usage it becomes expensive quickly.

Best for: Developers, API integration, business workflows, high-quality PDF and Office conversion, technically demanding format pairs.

Smallpdf — best for PDF-heavy workflows

Smallpdf is built entirely around PDF. It does PDF compression, PDF to Word, PDF to JPG, PDF merging, splitting, signing, e-signatures, and more — all with a very polished UI. If your work revolves around PDFs, Smallpdf's suite of tools is coherent and well-integrated.

Free tier: Two PDF tasks per day without an account. Simple tasks like compression or splitting a small file are fast. Anything heavier — large batches, OCR, password-protected files — requires a Pro account.

Privacy: Files are uploaded and processed server-side. Smallpdf uses AES-256 encryption in transit and at rest, and files are deleted after processing. They do offer a "Safe Mode" that processes files locally using client-side JavaScript for supported operations — a smart feature.

Pricing: Pro is around $12/month. A team plan is available. There's no permanent free tier for heavy users.

Best for: PDF-centric work, professional PDF editing, e-signatures, businesses that deal with contracts and documents.

ILovePDF — free PDF tools with a clean interface

ILovePDF positions itself as a more accessible Smallpdf alternative. It covers the common PDF operations — merge, split, compress, convert — with a simple drag-and-drop interface and a more generous free tier than Smallpdf. The sister site ILoveIMG handles image tasks.

Free tier: Most tools are free with file size limits (typically 50–100 MB). No daily conversion cap on most operations. Account required for some advanced features.

Privacy: Server-side processing with TLS encryption. Files are deleted within two hours. No advertising use of your file content.

Pricing: Premium at around $6–8/month for larger files, batch mode, and no watermarks on some outputs.

Best for: Casual PDF users who want more free conversions than Smallpdf allows, teams on a budget.

Adobe Express — best for design, worst for privacy

Adobe Express (formerly Adobe Spark) includes basic file conversion features, but it's primarily a design tool. PDF to JPG, PDF to Word, image resizing — these are all available, but Adobe's real pitch is templates, social media graphics, and brand kits.

Free tier: Available, but most conversion features require an Adobe account. Adobe uses your activity data for product improvement, and their privacy policy is extensive.

Privacy: Adobe processes files on its servers and is subject to US data handling rules. For personal users this is typically fine; for businesses in regulated industries, Adobe's data handling should be reviewed carefully.

Pricing: Free tier with Adobe account. Premium features bundled into Adobe Creative Cloud plans.

Best for: Users already in the Adobe ecosystem, design-adjacent conversion tasks, teams using Creative Cloud.

Zamzar — the veteran, showing its age

Zamzar launched in 2006 and used to send conversion results by email — a workflow that shows its age. It still works and still supports a wide format range, but the UX feels dated compared to Convertio or CloudConvert, and the free tier is limited to 50 MB files.

Best for: Legacy format pairs that newer tools don't support. Mostly superseded by Convertio for new users.

Desi Jugad runs entirely in your browser. The conversion happens on your device using WebAssembly ports of the same open-source libraries that power desktop tools — libheif, pdf.js, jsPDF, SheetJS, js-yaml, and others.

Privacy: Your files never leave your device. There is no upload step. You can verify this by opening your browser's Network tab — no outgoing file transfer will appear during conversion. This is the only category where no other tool on this list matches us.

Format support: We're honest about the tradeoff: browser-based conversion supports fewer format pairs than server-based tools. We don't handle video transcoding, OCR, CAD files, or rare legacy formats.

AI Studio: We also offer an AI Studio where you bring your own Replicate or Suno API key and access 20+ AI models at direct API cost — no markup.

Which tool should you choose?

The honest answer depends on your situation:

  • You're converting personal photos (HEIC, JPG, PNG, WebP): Use Desi Jugad. Your photos stay on your device, there are no limits, and it's instant.
  • You need to convert PDF to Word, or Word to PDF, at scale: CloudConvert (best quality) or ILovePDF (most generous free tier).

  • You need a rare or obscure format pair: Convertio's 300+ format library is unmatched.
  • You're a developer building a conversion pipeline: CloudConvert's API is the best-documented.

A note on privacy marketing

Several tools claim "secure" conversion and display padlocks prominently. Almost all of them mean "we use HTTPS" — which is a transport security measure, not a privacy guarantee. HTTPS encrypts data in transit. It says nothing about what happens to your file on the server after it arrives.

The only way to make a provable privacy guarantee is to eliminate the server from the conversion path entirely. That's what browser-based conversion does. When a tool says "your files are encrypted" and "deleted within X hours," those are commitments that depend on trusting the operator. When a tool's converter runs in your browser with no upload, there is nothing to trust — the file literally doesn't go anywhere.

Frequently asked questions

Does Convertio upload my files to a server?

Yes. Convertio processes files on its servers and deletes them after 24 hours. Files are transferred over HTTPS, but Convertio's infrastructure does receive and handle your files. This is standard for server-based converters.

Is CloudConvert free to use?

CloudConvert offers 25 free conversion minutes per day. Many conversions finish in seconds, so you can do several for free. Video conversion, large PDFs, or OCR tasks use more minutes. For heavy use, paid plans start around $8/month.

Does Smallpdf require a sign-up?

Basic use (two PDF tasks per day) doesn't require an account. For batch processing, larger files, or unlimited use, Smallpdf Pro is around $12/month.

What is the best Convertio alternative that doesn't upload files?

Desi Jugad for the most common format conversions (images, data, documents, text tools). Squoosh by Google for single-image compression. Neither supports the full format range of Convertio, but for privacy-sensitive files they're the only options where you can verify no upload happens.

Is ILovePDF safe?

ILovePDF uses TLS encryption and deletes files within two hours. It's a legitimate, established service that's safe for most use cases. For highly confidential documents, browser-based tools are a safer choice simply because there's no server exposure.
