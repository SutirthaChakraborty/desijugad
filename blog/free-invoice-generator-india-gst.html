Every freelancer, consultant, small business, and sole proprietor in India needs to issue invoices. For GST-registered businesses, those invoices must comply with specific GST rules. For those not registered for GST, a professional proforma invoice still matters for getting paid on time and maintaining records. Our free invoice generator creates both — downloadable as PDF, no subscription, no Tally licence needed.

What a valid GST invoice must contain

Under the CGST Act, a tax invoice issued by a GST-registered supplier must include:

  1. Name, address, and GSTIN of the supplier
  2. Invoice number (consecutive, unique per financial year)
  3. Invoice date
  4. Name, address, and GSTIN of the recipient (for B2B)
  5. HSN code or SAC code for each item/service
  6. Description of goods or services
  7. Quantity and unit of measure
  8. Unit price and total value before tax
  9. Applicable GST rate (CGST + SGST for intra-state, or IGST for inter-state)
  10. Tax amount (CGST, SGST, or IGST separately)
  11. Total invoice value
  12. Place of supply
  13. Signature or digital signature of the supplier

What if you're not registered for GST?

If your annual turnover is below the GST threshold (₹40 lakh for goods, ₹20 lakh for services in most states), you're not required to register for GST and should not charge GST. In this case:

  • Issue a Bill of Supply or Proforma Invoice — not a Tax Invoice.
  • Do not include GST or a GSTIN on the invoice.
  • Include your PAN as an identifier if the recipient requests it for their records.

Our invoice generator supports both modes — toggle the GST fields off for non-GST invoices.

HSN and SAC codes: what to use

HSN (Harmonized System of Nomenclature) codes are for goods. SAC (Services Accounting Code) codes are for services. They must appear on tax invoices. Common ones for freelancers and consultants:

  • SAC 998314 — IT software development services
  • SAC 998313 — IT consulting services
  • SAC 998361 — Digital content creation
  • SAC 998411 — Advertising and marketing services
  • SAC 998511 — Accounting and bookkeeping services
  • SAC 999299 — Educational support services

For most services: use 6-digit SAC codes. Look up the full list on the GST Council website.

Invoice numbering best practices

  • GST requires sequential invoice numbers within a financial year (April to March).
  • Format example: INV/2025-26/001, INV/2025-26/002, etc.
  • You can use alphanumeric codes but must not have gaps or duplicates.
  • Maintain a register (even a simple spreadsheet) of issued invoices.
