UK VAT Calculator 2026 — How to Add & Remove VAT

Published April 28, 2026 · 8 min read

VAT (Value Added Tax) is a consumption tax charged at 20% on most goods and services in the UK. Whether you're a freelancer raising invoices, a business calculating prices, or a consumer checking if you've been overcharged — understanding VAT is essential. This guide covers everything with a free VAT calculator.

UK VAT Rates 2026

  • Standard rate: 20% — applies to most goods and services
  • Reduced rate: 5% — domestic fuel and power, children's car seats, some energy-saving home improvements, certain social housing work
  • Zero rate: 0% — most food, children's clothing and footwear, books and newspapers, prescription medicines, some medical equipment, public transport
  • Exempt: insurance, education, health services (no VAT, no input tax recovery)

How to Add VAT to a Price (UK)

Formula: Gross price = Net price × 1.20 (for 20% VAT)

Example: You sell a service for £500 (net). VAT = £500 × 0.20 = £100. Total invoice = £600.

For 5% reduced rate: Net × 1.05. For zero rate: Net × 1.00 (no change).

How to Remove VAT from a Price (Reverse VAT)

When you have a VAT-inclusive price and need to find the net amount:

Formula: Net price = Gross price ÷ 1.20

Example: You paid £240 for a hotel room. Net = £240 ÷ 1.20 = £200. VAT = £40.

Important: Do NOT calculate VAT as 20% of the gross price. £240 × 20% = £48 is WRONG. Always divide by 1.20 for reverse VAT.

VAT Registration in the UK

  • You must register for VAT if your taxable turnover exceeds £90,000 in the previous 12 months (2024-25 threshold)
  • You can voluntarily register if your turnover is below the threshold
  • Once registered, you must charge VAT, submit VAT returns (usually quarterly), and can reclaim VAT on business purchases

VAT on Freelance Invoices

If you're VAT-registered, your invoice must show:

  • Your VAT registration number
  • Net amount (excl. VAT)
  • VAT amount charged (and the rate)
  • Gross total (incl. VAT)

Making Tax Digital (MTD)

HMRC's Making Tax Digital (MTD) requires VAT-registered businesses to keep digital records and submit VAT returns using compatible software. If you're above the £90,000 threshold, MTD for VAT is mandatory.

EU VAT after Brexit

Since Brexit (January 2021), UK VAT and EU VAT operate separately. Key points:

  • Goods exported to EU are zero-rated in the UK (but EU VAT may apply at the destination)
  • UK businesses selling to EU consumers may need to register for VAT in each EU country or use the EU's One Stop Shop (OSS)
  • The EU VAT area does not include the UK. UK VAT registration and EU VAT registration are separate

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the current UK VAT rate?

The standard UK VAT rate is 20%. It has been at 20% since January 4, 2011. The reduced rate is 5% and applies to a limited range of goods.

Do I need to pay VAT if I'm below the threshold?

No. If your taxable turnover is below £90,000, you don't need to register for VAT or charge it to customers. You also can't reclaim VAT on your purchases.

Is VAT charged on digital services?

Yes. Digital services (software, apps, e-books, streaming) sold to UK consumers are subject to UK VAT at 20%, regardless of where the supplier is based.
