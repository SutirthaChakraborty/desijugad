How to calculate EMI on home loan, car loan in India (2026)

9 min read·April 2026

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Instalment — the fixed amount you pay every month until your loan is fully repaid. Whether you're taking a home loan from SBI, a car loan from HDFC, or a personal loan from a fintech, understanding how EMI is calculated helps you negotiate better, plan your budget, and avoid being surprised by the total interest paid.

The EMI formula

Indian banks use the reducing balance method. The formula is:

EMI = P × r × (1 + r)ⁿ / ((1 + r)ⁿ − 1)

Where:

  • P = Principal loan amount
  • r = Monthly interest rate = Annual rate / 12 / 100
  • n = Loan tenure in months

Example: ₹30 lakh home loan at 8.5% for 20 years

  • P = ₹30,00,000
  • r = 8.5 / 12 / 100 = 0.007083
  • n = 20 × 12 = 240 months
  • EMI = 30,00,000 × 0.007083 × (1.007083)²⁴⁰ / ((1.007083)²⁴⁰ − 1)
  • EMI = ₹26,035 per month
  • Total paid over 20 years = ₹62.5 lakh
  • Total interest = ₹32.5 lakh (more than the principal!)

Reading an amortization schedule

An amortization schedule breaks down each monthly payment into its interest and principal components. The most important insight: in the early months, most of your EMI pays interest, not principal. In the later months, the ratio reverses.

For a ₹30 lakh loan at 8.5% over 20 years:

  • Month 1: ₹21,250 interest + ₹4,785 principal = ₹26,035 total
  • Month 60: ₹19,950 interest + ₹6,085 principal
  • Month 120: ₹17,780 interest + ₹8,255 principal
  • Month 240 (last): ₹183 interest + ₹25,852 principal

This is why prepaying in the first 5 years saves far more interest than prepaying in the last 5 years — in the early years, most of your balance is principal that is accruing heavy interest.

How to reduce your EMI

  1. Increase the down payment: Every rupee of down payment reduces the principal. A 30% down payment vs 20% on a ₹50 lakh home reduces your EMI by ~₹4,300/month.
  2. Longer tenure: Extending from 15 to 20 years reduces EMI but increases total interest paid — only do this if you need the cash flow.
  3. Negotiate interest rate: Even 0.25% matters on a home loan. On ₹50 lakh over 20 years, 0.25% lower = savings of ~₹2.5 lakh in total interest.
  4. Balance transfer: If your current rate is 1%+ higher than market, refinancing at a lower rate can reduce EMI significantly. Consider the processing fee (0.5–1%) in your calculation.
  5. Part prepayment: Most Indian home loans allow prepayment with no penalty. Even ₹50,000 extra in the 2nd year can save lakhs over the full tenure.
