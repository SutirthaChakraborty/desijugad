QR code scanner online free — scan from image or camera (2026)

6 min read·April 2026

Your phone camera scans QR codes natively — but what if you have a screenshot on your laptop, a QR code in a PDF, or a QR embedded in an image you received on WhatsApp? These are harder to scan with a phone. An online QR scanner lets you decode any QR code directly from your computer — no app, no installation.

When you need an online QR scanner

  • QR code is in a screenshot, PDF, or image file on your computer
  • QR received via email or message on desktop — can't easily point a phone at the screen
  • You want to verify what data a QR actually contains before scanning with your phone
  • Testing a QR code you just created — see the decoded content
  • Decoding a QR from a printed document you've photographed

What QR codes actually contain

QR codes are a 2D barcode format that can encode up to 4,296 alphanumeric characters. Common encodings you'll encounter:

  • URL: https://example.com — opens a website
  • UPI: upi://pay?pa=abc@upi&pn=Name&am=100 — payment QR codes from Paytm, PhonePe, BHIM
  • WiFi: WIFI:S:NetworkName;T:WPA;P:Password;; — connects to WiFi automatically
  • vCard: Contact information in VCard format
  • SMS: Pre-filled SMS message
  • Email: Pre-addressed email
  • Plain text: Any text content
  • geo: Location coordinates

UPI payment QR codes are especially common in India — every small shop has one. The online scanner decodes the UPI ID and payment amount so you can verify before paying.

How to use the online QR scanner

  1. Open the QR Code Scanner.
  2. Choose your input method:
  • Upload image: Drop a JPG, PNG, WebP, or screenshot containing the QR code. The scanner detects the QR automatically even if it's not the entire image.
  • Webcam: Click "Use Camera" — point your laptop camera at a physical QR code or a phone screen showing a QR.
  • Paste from clipboard: Ctrl+V to paste a screenshot directly.
  1. The decoded content appears immediately — URL is clickable, WiFi credentials shown clearly, vCard formatted.

Security: why to check QR codes before scanning

QR phishing (quishing) is a growing threat. Attackers replace legitimate QR codes with malicious ones. Examples:

  • Stickers placed over legitimate UPI QR codes at shops — redirecting payments to attacker's account
  • Fake parking tickets with QR codes leading to credential-harvesting sites
  • Email attachments with QR codes bypassing URL filters
  • QR codes in phishing emails disguised as invoice PDFs

Best practice: before scanning an unknown QR with your phone (which might auto-open the URL), use the online scanner to first see the raw URL. If it's a suspicious domain, don't proceed.
