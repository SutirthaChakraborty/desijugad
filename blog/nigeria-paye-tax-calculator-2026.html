Nigeria PAYE Tax Calculator 2026 — Net Salary & Income Tax Guide

Published April 28, 2026 · 9 min read

Nigeria's Pay As You Earn (PAYE) system is governed by the Personal Income Tax Act (PITA), as amended by the Finance Acts of 2019, 2020, and 2021. Administered jointly by state Internal Revenue Services (IRS) — since employment income is taxed by the state where you work — PAYE is the primary way most Nigerian employees pay income tax. Understanding how it's calculated helps you verify your payslip and plan your finances.

Nigeria PAYE Tax Table for 2025-2026

Nigeria uses a progressive tax system with the following bands on annual taxable income:

Minimum tax rule: Where total income tax payable is less than 1% of gross income, the tax payable is 1% of gross income. This ensures everyone earning above the relief threshold contributes at least minimally.

Consolidated Relief Allowance (CRA): Nigeria's Key Tax Relief

Before applying tax rates, every Nigerian employee is entitled to a Consolidated Relief Allowance (CRA) that reduces taxable income. The CRA is the higher of:

  • ₦200,000 per year + 20% of gross income, OR
  • 1% of gross income

For most employees, the first formula (₦200,000 + 20% of gross) gives a larger relief. For example, on an annual income of ₦3,000,000: CRA = ₦200,000 + 20% × ₦3,000,000 = ₦200,000 + ₦600,000 = ₦800,000. Taxable income = ₦3,000,000 – ₦800,000 = ₦2,200,000.

Other Statutory Deductions Before Tax

  • Employee Pension Contribution: 8% of monthly emolument (basic salary + housing allowance + transport allowance). Fully deductible from taxable income.
  • National Housing Fund (NHF): 2.5% of basic salary, deductible.
  • Life assurance premiums: Deductible.
  • National Health Insurance Scheme (NHIS): Employee contribution is deductible.

Important: Pension contributions under the Pension Reform Act 2014 are tax-exempt. The employer contributes 10% and the employee contributes 8% minimum. The employer's contribution is a benefit-in-kind but is also exempt from tax.

Exempt Income and Allowances

Certain components of your employment package are completely exempt from PAYE:

  • Gratuities paid on retirement
  • Retirement benefits from approved pension/provident funds
  • Compensation for loss of employment (up to specified limits)
  • Medical expenses reimbursed by employer (at cost, no ceiling)
  • Passages (travel costs for work)
  • Interest on government securities

Which State Collects Your PAYE?

A unique feature of Nigerian PAYE: employment income is taxed by the state where you perform your duties, not where you live. If you work in Lagos but live in Ogun State, your PAYE goes to Lagos Internal Revenue Service (LIRS). This matters when:

  • You work remotely from a different state than your employer is registered
  • You have multiple employment income sources in different states
  • You relocate mid-year

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my net salary after PAYE in Nigeria?

Start with gross income, deduct pension (8% of emolument), NHF (2.5% of basic), and other statutory deductions. From the result, subtract the CRA (₦200,000 + 20% of gross or 1% of gross, whichever is higher). Apply the PAYE bands to the remaining taxable income. Subtract from gross to get approximate net. When are PAYE returns due in Nigeria?

Employers must remit monthly PAYE deductions to the relevant State IRS by the 10th of the following month. Annual employer returns (Form H1) are due by January 31 of the following year. Individual employees with additional income file personal income tax returns by March 31.

What is the minimum PAYE in Nigeria?

If your calculated PAYE is less than 1% of gross income, you pay 1% of gross income as minimum tax. For example, if gross income is ₦1,000,000 and calculated tax is ₦5,000 (less than 1% = ₦10,000), you pay ₦10,000. This ensures low-income earners with high reliefs still contribute.
