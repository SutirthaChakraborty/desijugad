Nigeria PAYE Tax Calculator 2026 — Net Salary & Income Tax Guide

Published April 28, 2026 · 9 min read

PAYE (Pay As You Earn) is Nigeria's income tax system for employees. Every month, your employer deducts income tax, pension, NHF, and sometimes NHIS from your gross salary before you receive your net pay. This guide explains how each deduction works and how to calculate your take-home salary.

PAYE Tax Rates for Nigeria 2026

Nigeria uses progressive PAYE rates on chargeable income (gross salary minus Consolidated Relief Allowance):

  • 7% on first ₦600,000 of chargeable income per year
  • 11% on next ₦500,000
  • 15% on next ₦500,000
  • 19% on next ₦500,000
  • 21% on next ₦1,600,000
  • 24% on income above ₦3,200,000

Consolidated Relief Allowance (CRA)

Before applying PAYE rates, you deduct the CRA from your gross income. CRA = the higher of: ₦200,000 or 1% of gross income, PLUS 20% of gross income.

Example: For ₦3,000,000 annual gross income:

  • 1% of gross = ₦30,000. Higher of ₦30,000 or ₦200,000 = ₦200,000
  • 20% of gross = ₦600,000
  • Total CRA = ₦200,000 + ₦600,000 = ₦800,000
  • Chargeable income = ₦3,000,000 - ₦800,000 = ₦2,200,000

Pension Deduction (Contributory Pension Scheme)

Under the Pension Reform Act (PRA) 2004:

  • Employee contribution: 8% of monthly gross emolument
  • Employer contribution: minimum 10% of monthly gross emolument
  • Monthly gross emolument = basic salary + housing allowance + transport allowance
  • Pension contributions are tax-deductible (reduce your taxable income)

National Housing Fund (NHF)

NHF is deducted from employees in the formal sector:

  • Rate: 2.5% of monthly basic salary
  • Purpose: Contributes to the Federal Mortgage Bank of Nigeria housing fund
  • NHF is refundable (with interest) when you retire, relocate abroad permanently, or die

NHIS (National Health Insurance Scheme)

Currently voluntary for private sector employees:

  • Employee contribution: 5% of basic monthly salary
  • Employer contribution: 10% of basic monthly salary

Example Salary Calculation (Nigeria)

Scenario: Annual gross salary = ₦5,000,000

  • CRA = ₦200,000 + (20% × ₦5,000,000) = ₦1,200,000
  • Pension (8%) = ₦400,000
  • Chargeable income = ₦5,000,000 - ₦1,200,000 - ₦400,000 = ₦3,400,000
  • PAYE tax (progressive) ≈ ₦620,000
  • NHF (2.5% of basic) ≈ ₦60,000
  • Net take-home ≈ ₦3,920,000/year ≈ ₦326,000/month

Nigeria vs Other Countries — Tax Burden Comparison

  • Nigeria: Effective rate ~15-20% for middle income. Progressive but CRA provides significant relief.
  • India: Effective rate ~5-15% for same income level with new regime and 87A rebate.
  • UK: Effective rate ~25-35% including National Insurance. Higher rates, better public services.
  • UAE: 0% income tax. No deductions from salary at all.
  • Kenya: Similar to Nigeria in structure but uses PAYE with NHIF and NSSF deductions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is liable for PAYE in Nigeria?

All individuals employed in Nigeria (citizens and expatriates) with income sourced from Nigeria. State Internal Revenue Services (SIRS) collect PAYE. Your employer must remit it by the 10th of every month.

What is the minimum taxable income in Nigeria?

The CRA mechanism effectively makes the first ₦800,000 of annual gross income (approximately) tax-free for many employees. Exact figures depend on salary structure and allowance composition.

Can I claim a tax refund in Nigeria?

Yes, if excess PAYE was deducted. File a tax return with your SIRS and request a refund or credit note. This is rare for salaried employees but can happen if you lose your job mid-year.
