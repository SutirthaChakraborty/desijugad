What is EMI and How is It Calculated?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount you pay every month to repay a loan. It includes both principal (the borrowed amount) and interest. The EMI formula is: P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1] where P = principal, r = monthly interest rate, n = number of months.

Why Use a Loan Calculator?

Manual EMI calculation is complex and error-prone. A loan calculator instantly shows:

  • Monthly EMI payment amount
  • Total interest you'll pay
  • Complete amortization schedule
  • Principal vs interest breakdown month-by-month

Types of Loans You Can Calculate

Home Loan (Mortgage)

Typically 15-20 year tenure at 8-9% interest in India. Example: ₹30 lakh at 8.5% for 20 years = ₹2,82,868 EMI

Auto Loan

Usually 5-7 years at 9-12% interest. Example: ₹10 lakh at 10% for 5 years = ₹21,247 EMI

Personal Loan

Shorter tenure (3-5 years) at higher rates (12-15%). Example: ₹5 lakh at 13% for 3 years = ₹16,315 EMI

Education Loan

Longer tenure (5-15 years) at lower rates (7-10%) with moratorium period options.

Pro Tips for Loan Borrowing

  • Shorter tenure = less total interest but higher EMI. Find your comfort zone.
  • Even 0.5% lower interest saves significant money over time
  • Make part-prepayments if possible to reduce tenure and interest
  • Compare fixed vs floating rates based on market conditions

Conclusion
