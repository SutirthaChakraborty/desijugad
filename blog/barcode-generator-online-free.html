Understanding EAN-13 structure (for Indian products)

EAN-13 barcodes have a specific structure:

  • First 3 digits: GS1 country prefix — India is 890. Products sold in India should start with 890.
  • Next 4–9 digits: Company prefix — assigned by GS1 India when you register.
  • Next 3–8 digits: Item reference — you assign these per product.
  • Last 1 digit: Check digit — calculated automatically from the previous 12 digits.

Important: For products sold in retail chains, you must register with GS1 India to get an official company prefix. The barcodes our tool generates are structurally valid but not registered in any global database — they're suitable for internal use, testing, and small-scale retail where no POS system looks up the barcode in a database.

PNG vs SVG: which to download?

  • PNG: Raster format. Best for: printing on label printers, embedding in Word/Excel, attaching to emails. Use at high resolution (300 DPI minimum for print).
  • SVG: Vector format. Best for: laser engraving, professional printing, embedding in websites, scaling to any size without blur. Use SVG whenever possible for quality.

Minimum size for barcode printing

Barcodes that are too small fail to scan. Minimum recommended sizes:

  • EAN-13: minimum width 37.29mm × height 25.93mm at 100% magnification
  • Code 128: minimum height 15mm; width depends on data length
  • QR Code: minimum 2cm × 2cm for standard scanning distance

Thermal label printers (Zebra, TSC, SATO) are the standard for barcode labels — they produce the consistent contrast and resolution that scanners require.
