Convert GIF to Video — MP4, WebM, MKV Converter Free

April 27, 2026 • 4 min read

GIF was invented in 1987, and while it's still everywhere, it's showing its age. A 5-second animated GIF of a screen recording might be 15–20 MB. The same clip as an MP4 would be under 1 MB. GIF supports only 256 colours per frame — enough for simple logos but terrible for anything photographic. And GIF has no audio. Converting your GIFs to video solves all of these problems at once.

Why GIF to Video Is Always Worth It

The numbers tell the story. Here's a real comparison for the same 5-second animation clip:

The quality improvement is as dramatic as the size reduction. MP4 renders the same frames with full colour depth, without the visible banding and dithering artifacts that plague GIFs.

When to Convert GIF to Video

  • Website performance: Large GIFs are one of the most common causes of slow page loads. Replace them with autoplay silent looping MP4 videos (<video autoplay muted loop playsinline>) for dramatically faster load times. Google's PageSpeed Insights specifically flags GIFs and recommends this conversion.
  • Social media uploads: TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube don't accept GIF uploads directly. Converting to MP4 is required to post animated content to these platforms.
  • Adding audio: A GIF is silent by nature. Convert to video first, then add an audio track using a video editor to create a complete multimedia clip.
  • Storage optimisation: If you have a collection of old GIFs taking up space, converting them to MP4 can reduce their combined size by 80–90%.
  • Video editing workflows: Most professional video editors (Premiere Pro, DaVinci Resolve, Final Cut) handle MP4 natively but may have issues with GIF. Convert first to bring animated content into your video project.

Understanding the Output Format Options

  • MP4 (H.264): Best compatibility. Plays everywhere — Windows, macOS, iOS, Android, smart TVs. Choose MP4 when you're unsure where the video will be played.
  • WebM (VP9): Best for web. Smaller file size than MP4, supported in all modern browsers. Ideal for website background videos and web apps. Not supported natively on iOS (use MP4 for iPhone compatibility).
  • MKV: Best for archiving. Stores the video stream with maximum quality and can be remuxed to other containers later without re-encoding. Use MKV for long-term storage when you might want to convert again in the future.

How to Use GIF-to-Video for Web Performance

If you're a web developer looking to improve page speed by replacing GIFs, here's the recommended HTML pattern after conversion:

<video autoplay muted loop playsinline width="480" height="270">
  <source src="animation.webm" type="video/webm">
  <source src="animation.mp4" type="video/mp4">
</video>

The playsinline attribute is essential for iOS Safari autoplay. Providing both WebM and MP4 sources ensures full browser compatibility — WebM for Chrome/Firefox/Edge, MP4 as the fallback for Safari.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will converting GIF to MP4 improve the visual quality?

Yes — significantly for photographic or complex coloured GIFs. The 256-colour limit of GIF causes visible banding and dithering. MP4 uses full colour depth (16.7 million colours), so gradients and photographs look dramatically better. For simple flat-colour logos, the difference is minor.

Does the converted video loop like the original GIF?

The looping behaviour must be set in the player, not the file. In HTML, add the loop attribute to your <video> tag. On social media platforms, short clips often loop automatically. The MP4 file itself doesn't contain loop instructions — that was a GIF-specific feature.

Can I add audio to the GIF after converting it to video?

Yes. Once you have the MP4, use a video editor (or our Video Converter) to add an audio track. The conversion step creates the video container; adding audio requires a separate edit step.
