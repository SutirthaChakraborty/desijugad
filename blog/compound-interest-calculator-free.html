What is Compound Interest (The 8th Wonder of the World)?

Albert Einstein called compound interest the 8th wonder of the world. Compound interest is "interest on interest" — your earnings generate their own earnings, creating exponential growth. Unlike simple interest (which only calculates on the principal), compound interest accelerates your wealth over time.

The Power of Time + Compounding

Example: ₹1 lakh at 12% annual return:

  • After 5 years: ₹1.76 lakh (76% gain)
  • After 10 years: ₹3.11 lakh (211% gain)
  • After 20 years: ₹9.65 lakh (865% gain!)

Notice how doubling the time period (10 to 20 years) more than triples the wealth. This is compound interest at work.

Compounding Frequency Matters

The same ₹1 lakh at 12% for 10 years, with different compounding:

  • Annually: ₹3.11 lakh
  • Quarterly: ₹3.24 lakh
  • Monthly: ₹3.28 lakh
  • Daily: ₹3.32 lakh

More frequent compounding = more wealth. This is why bank fixed deposits compound quarterly or monthly, not annually.

Compound Interest Investment Options in India

Bank Fixed Deposits (FDs)

Safe, government-backed, 5-7% returns. Compounds quarterly. Best for conservative investors.

Mutual Funds (SIPs)

Historically 10-14% annual returns. Compounds daily. Best for long-term wealth building (10+ years).

National Savings Certificate (NSC)

7.4% returns, government-backed. Compounds annually. Good for 5-year lockup.

Recurring Deposits (RDs)

5-7% returns on monthly deposits. Compounds quarterly. Good for regular savers.

Rule of 72 — Quick Growth Estimator

Want to know how long to double your money? Divide 72 by your interest rate.

At 12% return: 72 ÷ 12 = 6 years to double your money

At 8% return: 72 ÷ 8 = 9 years to double your money

Start Early for Maximum Wealth

Investing ₹500/month from age 25:

  • At 12% returns for 35 years = ₹2.68 crore at age 60
  • Total invested: only ₹21 lakh
  • Earnings from compound interest: ₹2.47 crore!

The takeaway: Time in market beats timing the market. Start investing early, stay consistent, and let compound interest do the heavy lifting.

Conclusion
