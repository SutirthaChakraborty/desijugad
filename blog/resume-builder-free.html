Why Your Resume Format Matters

Recruiters spend 6 seconds scanning a resume. If yours looks unprofessional or hard to read, it gets rejected before they even see your skills. A well-formatted resume with consistent fonts, clear sections, and strategic spacing dramatically increases interview callbacks.

What Makes a Great Resume

1. Clear Structure

  • Name and contact info at top
  • Professional summary (optional, 2-3 lines)
  • Work experience (most recent first)
  • Education
  • Skills

2. Professional Formatting

  • Clean font (Arial, Calibri, or Georgia)
  • Consistent spacing and margins
  • Single page for 5 years experience, max 2 pages
  • No graphics, colors, or design elements (ATS hostile)

3. Action Verbs and Metrics

Bad: "Responsible for managing social media"
Good: "Increased Instagram followers from 5K to 50K (900% growth) through content strategy and influencer partnerships"

Content Tips by Section

Professional Summary

2-3 lines about who you are and what you do. Tailor it for each job you apply to.

Example: "Data-driven marketer with 5 years experience growing B2B SaaS revenue from $500K to $3M through performance marketing and brand strategy."

Work Experience

For each job, include:

  • Job title
  • Company name
  • Dates (month/year)
  • 3-5 bullet points of achievements (with numbers where possible)

Lead with your biggest achievement, not daily tasks.

Education

  • Degree and major
  • University name
  • Graduation year
  • GPA (if 3.5+, otherwise skip)

Skills

List 6-10 relevant skills. Prioritize by job requirement.

Resume Mistakes That Hurt You

  • ❌ Typos and grammar errors — Recruiters interpret this as careless
  • ❌ Irrelevant information — "5 years old: Learned to ride bicycle"
  • ❌ Unexplained employment gaps — If unemployed, explain it briefly
  • ❌ Personal pronouns (I, me, we) — Use action verbs instead
  • ❌ Objective statement (dated) — Replace with professional summary
  • ❌ Photo (unless requested) — Can invite bias

ATS Optimization (Getting Past the Bots)

Most companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) that scan for keywords before humans see your resume.

To optimize for ATS:

  • Use standard formatting (no tables, text boxes, graphics)
  • Mirror keywords from the job description
  • Use standard section headings
  • Save as PDF or DOC (not fancy formats)
  • Avoid creative fonts and colors

How Often to Update Your Resume

Update your resume:

  • After each job change
  • When you get a promotion or raise
  • When you complete relevant courses
  • Before each job application (tailor it!)
