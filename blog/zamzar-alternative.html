The best free Zamzar alternative in 2026 — no email, no file upload

7 min read · Updated May 2026

You open Zamzar, drag in your file, pick an output format, and then — there it is — an input box asking for your email address. You can't skip it. You won't get your converted file unless you hand over your email. That's not a bug or an oversight; it's deliberate. And for a lot of people converting personal documents, business files, or anything remotely sensitive, it's a dealbreaker.

This article is from the team at Desi Jugad, so we'll be upfront: we built a Zamzar alternative. We'll give you an honest assessment of when Zamzar is the right tool anyway, when you should use something else, and exactly what makes Desi Jugad different.

Why people look for Zamzar alternatives

Zamzar launched in 2006 — making it one of the oldest file converters on the internet. At the time, it was genuinely innovative: drag in a file, enter your email, come back later to find the converted result in your inbox. Broadband was slow, smartphones barely existed, and client-side conversion was technically impossible. The email workflow made sense.

It's 2026. None of those constraints exist. Modern browsers can run the same conversion libraries that power desktop software. Files can be converted in milliseconds without ever leaving your device. And yet Zamzar still asks for your email address. Every. Single. Time.

The practical consequences are real:

  • Privacy risk for sensitive files. Your file travels to Zamzar's UK servers. Your email is collected and associated with that file. If you're converting a tax document, a contract, medical records, or unreleased creative work, you've now handed over both the file content and an identifier linked to you personally.
  • Inbox noise. Zamzar will use your email address to market paid plans to you. Some users report persistent email sequences even after one-time use.
  • Friction for a one-off task. Sometimes you just need to convert a PNG to ICO for a project and you don't want an ongoing relationship with a file conversion service.
  • 50 MB free limit. The free tier caps file size at 50 MB and allows only 3 simultaneous conversions. For a modern RAW photo, a presentation, or a short video clip, 50 MB is nothing.

When Zamzar is still the right tool

Zamzar has been around long enough to accumulate support for genuinely obscure formats that newer converters haven't bothered with. If you need to convert any of the following, Zamzar is worth the email address:

  • CAD formats — DWG, DXF, and other AutoCAD files
  • Legacy video — WMV, RealMedia (.rm, .rmvb), older container formats
  • Older audio formats — FLAC to M4A, OGG conversions, AIFF handling
  • Old Office formats — WPS (Works), older Publisher files
  • eBook formats — LIT, MOBI, PDB

For any of these, Zamzar's 18-year format library is genuinely useful. Just use a throwaway or secondary email address, or an email alias, if you don't want your primary inbox exposed.

Frequently asked questions

Why does Zamzar ask for my email address?

Zamzar was founded in 2006 when the only practical delivery mechanism for large converted files was email. The product was originally built around that workflow: upload, wait, receive by email. Despite browser capabilities making this unnecessary, Zamzar kept the email requirement — it's also how they qualify leads for paid plan upsells. There is no technical reason a modern converter needs your email address to deliver a file.

Is there a Zamzar alternative that doesn't require email or sign-up?

Yes. Desi Jugad converts 130+ file formats entirely in your browser — no email, no account, no file upload to any server. Convertio and CloudConvert are also email-free for basic use, but they do upload files to remote servers. For privacy-sensitive files, browser-based conversion is the only option where you can verify no upload occurs.

Does Zamzar store my files?

Zamzar uploads your files to its servers in the UK and stores them for up to 24 hours before deletion. Your email address is retained separately and may be used for marketing. Paid users get longer file retention periods. On Desi Jugad, files are never uploaded at all — the conversion runs locally, so there is nothing to store or delete.

What is the file size limit on Zamzar free?

Zamzar's free tier limits individual files to 50 MB and allows 3 simultaneous conversions. Pro plans start at approximately $16/month and raise the limit to 400 MB. Desi Jugad has no file size limit — the only practical constraint is your device's available RAM, which for modern computers allows files well into the gigabyte range for image and document conversion.

When is Zamzar actually the better choice?

Zamzar's 18-year format library covers obscure and legacy formats that browser-based tools cannot handle: CAD files (DWG, DXF), old video containers (WMV, RealMedia), rare document formats, and eBook formats like LIT and MOBI. For anything outside common images, PDFs, and data files, Zamzar or Convertio are the practical choices — just use a secondary email address if privacy matters.
