TDS Calculator India — Tax Deducted at Source Free Guide 2025-26

Published April 28, 2026 · 8 min read

TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) is one of the most misunderstood parts of Indian taxation. Whether you're a salaried employee checking your payslip, a freelancer receiving professional fees, or a landlord collecting rent — TDS affects you. This guide explains TDS rates, sections, and gives you a free calculator to estimate your deductions.

What is TDS?

TDS stands for Tax Deducted at Source. Under the Income Tax Act, 1961, certain payers (employers, banks, companies) are required to deduct tax at the time of making payments and deposit it with the government. The recipient then claims this as a credit when filing their ITR.

Think of TDS as an advance tax collected by the payer on behalf of the government. It ensures tax collection happens throughout the year rather than in one lump sum at year-end.

Key TDS Rates for FY 2025-26

Section 192 — TDS on Salary

TDS on salary is not a flat percentage. Your employer calculates your estimated annual income, applies the income tax slab rates (under your chosen regime), subtracts eligible deductions, and deducts 1/12th of the total tax each month.

  • New tax regime: No standard deduction applicable (rebate u/s 87A applies up to ₹12 lakh income)
  • Old tax regime: Standard deduction of ₹50,000 + all Chapter VI-A deductions

Section 194A — TDS on Interest (Banks)

  • Rate: 10% (if PAN provided) / 20% (if PAN not provided)
  • Threshold: Interest > ₹40,000 per year (₹50,000 for senior citizens) from a single bank
  • Applicable to: Savings account interest, FD interest, recurring deposit interest

Section 194C — TDS on Contractor Payments

  • Individual/HUF: 1%
  • Others (companies, firms): 2%
  • Threshold: Single payment > ₹30,000 or cumulative > ₹1 lakh in a financial year

Section 194I — TDS on Rent

  • Land, building, furniture/fittings: 10%
  • Plant and machinery: 2%
  • Threshold: Annual rent > ₹2,40,000

Section 194J — TDS on Professional/Technical Fees

  • Professional fees (doctors, lawyers, engineers): 10%
  • Technical services: 2%
  • Threshold: > ₹30,000 per year per payee
  • Commonly applies to: Freelancers, consultants, CA/CS fees

Section 194N — TDS on Cash Withdrawals

  • 2% on withdrawals above ₹1 crore (if ITR filed)
  • 2% on withdrawals between ₹20 lakh – ₹1 crore (if ITR not filed for 3 years)
  • 5% on withdrawals above ₹1 crore (if ITR not filed)

Quick TDS Calculator

For FD interest TDS: If your FD interest from a bank exceeds ₹40,000 in a year, the bank will deduct 10% TDS. Example: ₹60,000 interest → TDS = ₹6,000 (10%).

For rent TDS: If annual rent is ₹3,00,000, TDS = ₹30,000 (10%). Monthly TDS deducted by tenant = ₹2,500.

How to Avoid Excess TDS Deduction

Submit Form 15G / 15H

If your total income is below the taxable limit:

  • Form 15G: For individuals below 60 years, income below ₹2.5 lakh. Submit to your bank to prevent TDS on FD interest.
  • Form 15H: For senior citizens (60+), income below ₹3 lakh. Same purpose.

Note: Submitting these forms when you're not eligible is a criminal offence.

Apply for Lower TDS Certificate (Section 197)

If you have legitimate reasons for lower TDS (e.g., business losses, advance tax payments), apply to your Assessing Officer for a certificate under Section 197. The payer then deducts TDS at the lower rate mentioned in the certificate.

Checking Your TDS in Form 26AS

  1. Log in to incometax.gov.in
  2. Go to e-File → Income Tax Returns → View Form 26AS
  3. Select the financial year
  4. View all TDS deductions against your PAN from all deductors

Always verify Form 26AS before filing ITR — mismatches between Form 26AS and your employer's TDS certificates (Form 16/16A) can cause ITR processing delays.

TDS vs Advance Tax — Key Difference

TDS is deducted by the payer. Advance Tax is paid by you directly. Both reduce your final income tax liability. If TDS + Advance Tax exceed your actual tax, you get a refund. If they fall short, you pay the difference when filing ITR.
