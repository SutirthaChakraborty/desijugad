Setup Guide

Desi Jugad AI Studio setup guide — Replicate key, Suno key, costs explained

12 min read · April 2026

Desi Jugad AI Studio works on a BYOK model — Bring Your Own Key. You connect your Replicate and Suno API keys directly, generation costs go to your account at their published rate, and Desi Jugad takes zero margin. This guide covers everything from signing up for Replicate to what to do if a generation gets stuck.

What is BYOK and why does it matter?

Most AI tools work like this: the company pays for API access in bulk, marks it up 3–10×, and charges you a subscription or per-generation fee. You get one model (or a small curated set), you pay their price, and you have no insight into what it actually costs.

BYOK is different. You sign up directly with the model provider (Replicate, Suno, Gemini) and connect your own account. When you generate an image or video, the API call goes from your browser directly to Replicate via a CORS proxy — a thin pass-through that never reads your key or your content. Desi Jugad never touches your generation, never stores your key, and takes no cut of the cost.

The advantages:

  • Actual pricing — FLUX Schnell costs $0.003 per image. That's ₹0.25. You pay that. Not $0.05 wrapped in a subscription.
  • Full model access — Replicate has over 1,200 models. You're not locked into whatever a SaaS vendor decided to feature.
  • Privacy — Your prompts and outputs go to Replicate (who is accountable for them), not to a middleman who also happens to run an ad business.
  • No subscription traps — Use it for a month, stop using it for three months, come back. No monthly charge just for having an account.

Part 1: Getting a Replicate API key

Replicate is the AI inference platform that powers the image, video, LLM, and photo studio tools on Desi Jugad. Here's how to get set up:

Step 1: Create a Replicate account

Go to replicate.com and sign up. You can use a GitHub account, Google account, or email. The signup takes about 90 seconds. You don't need to provide payment information immediately.

Step 2: Add a payment method

Go to replicate.com/account/billing and add a credit or debit card. Replicate requires this before you can run models. Indian Rupee billing works fine — they charge in USD and your card does the conversion at standard rates. Visa and Mastercard both work. UPI is not currently supported.

New accounts often receive some free credit automatically — typically $0.50 to $5 — which is enough for a few hundred images or a short video test.

Step 3: Create an API token

Go to replicate.com/account/api-tokens. Click "Create token". Give it a name (e.g., "Desi Jugad"). Copy the token — it starts with r8_ and is about 40 characters long.

Important: copy it now. Replicate only shows the full token once. If you lose it, you'll need to create a new one (the old one still works until you delete it).

Step 4: Paste it into Desi Jugad settings

Go to AI Settings. Find the Replicate section. Click the input field and paste your token. Click Save. The pill at the top of any AI tool page will turn green, confirming the key is set.

Part 2: Getting a Suno API key (for music generation)

Suno is the AI music generation service that powers the Music Studio. Unlike Replicate, Suno doesn't offer a public official API as of April 2026. The integration goes through a third-party wrapper service.

Desi Jugad defaults to sunoapi.org as the Suno wrapper. Here's how to get a key:

Step 1: Create a sunoapi.org account

Go to sunoapi.org and sign up. You'll need an email address. Verification takes a few minutes.

Step 2: Buy credits

Suno wrapper services work on a credits model. sunoapi.org sells credits in packages. At typical rates, generating a 30-second song costs 5–10 credits. A package of 1,000 credits costs around $3–5. This is significantly cheaper than Suno's official subscription, which is designed for casual consumers rather than API users.

Step 3: Get your API key

In your sunoapi.org dashboard, look for "API Keys" or "Developer". Copy your key — it usually starts with sk-.

Step 4: Paste it in settings

Go to AI Settings → Suno section. Paste your key and click Save. The credits remaining will appear on the Music Studio page once the key is set.

Alternative Suno wrappers

If sunoapi.org is unavailable or you prefer another service, Desi Jugad also supports kie.ai and piapi.ai as Suno providers. You can switch between them in Settings → Suno Provider. The API shapes are nearly identical across providers, so your existing prompts work without changes.

Part 3: Understanding the costs

The most common question: how much does this actually cost? Let's go through the main tools.

Image generation

Image generation is the cheapest AI tool by a large margin. Costs in April 2026:

  • FLUX Schnell — $0.003 per image (₹0.25). Fast, good quality. 100 images for 30 cents.
  • FLUX 1.1 Pro — $0.04 per image (₹3.35). Best overall quality. 25 images for $1.
  • Ideogram v3 Turbo — $0.03 per image (₹2.50). Excellent text rendering in images.
  • Recraft v3 — ~$0.04 per image. Best-in-class for design and illustration styles.

For casual use — generating 10–20 images to find the right one — you'll spend $0.30–$0.80. For a project that needs 200+ image variations, a few dollars.

Video generation

Video is significantly more expensive because it requires much more compute. Costs are per second of output:

  • Wan 2.7 — ~$0.04/sec (₹3/sec). Cheapest option, open source.
  • Veo 3.1 Lite — $0.10/sec (₹8/sec). Google, good quality at accessible cost.
  • Veo 3.1 Fast — $0.25/sec (₹21/sec). Cinematic quality, native audio.
  • Veo 3.1 — $0.50/sec (₹42/sec). Best quality, most expensive.
  • Seedance 2.0 — $0.14/sec (₹12/sec). Best for music videos, native audio.

For a single 8-second test clip with Veo 3.1 Fast, that's $2 (₹168). Always check the cost estimate in the tool before generating.

Music generation

Suno wrapper pricing varies by provider. Typical cost: $0.02–$0.05 per 30-second generation with sunoapi.org. A full 3-minute song generation with V5.5 costs around $0.10–$0.20 depending on the wrapper and any extension calls needed.

Writing (LLM)

LLM calls on Replicate are priced per 1,000 tokens (roughly 750 words). Typical costs:

  • Claude Sonnet 3.7 — ~$0.012/1K output tokens. A 1,000-word article costs about $0.015 (₹1.25).
  • Llama 3.3 70B — ~$0.0008/1K tokens. A 1,000-word article costs about $0.001 (₹0.08).
  • Mistral Large — ~$0.004/1K tokens. A 1,000-word article costs about $0.005 (₹0.40).

Part 4: How your key is stored and protected

This is worth understanding clearly.

Your API keys are stored in your browser's localStorage under the key dj_ai_keys_v1. localStorage is isolated per domain — only code running on desijugad.co.in can read it. Keys are stored as a JSON object, e.g., {"replicate":"r8_xxxx","suno":"sk-xxxx"}.

When you trigger a generation, the browser reads the key from localStorage and includes it in the Authorization header of a fetch request to /api/proxy?target=replicate&path=…. The Cloudflare Pages Function at that path reads the header and forwards it — literally copies the header value — to api.replicate.com. It does not log it, store it, or inspect it. The function code is a few dozen lines and you can read it in the GitHub repository.

The response from Replicate comes back through the same proxy with CORS headers added (so the browser accepts it), and arrives in your browser tab.

What this means: The key never travels to any server owned by Desi Jugad except as an opaque value in a header that's immediately forwarded. It's equivalent to using a browser extension that forwards your requests — except this code is open source and you can verify it.

One important caveat: storing API keys in browser localStorage is convenient but not the most secure storage possible. A malicious browser extension, or an XSS flaw in the site (we work hard to prevent this, but no site is immune), could read localStorage. For very high-value keys (enterprise Replicate tokens with large credit balances), consider using a Replicate key with a spending limit set in the Replicate dashboard. This limits potential damage even if the key were somehow exposed.

Part 5: What to do if a generation gets stuck

Replicate predictions occasionally get stuck — this can happen due to server load, model cold-start issues, or transient failures. Here's what to do:

Wait a few more minutes

Video and music predictions can take 3–7 minutes depending on model and queue. The timeout in Desi Jugad's tools is set at 5 minutes for images, 10 minutes for video, and 15 minutes for music video. If the tool shows "This is taking unusually long", it means the timeout was hit — but the Replicate prediction may still be running.

Check the Replicate dashboard

Go to replicate.com/deployments or replicate.com/p/ to see running and recent predictions. If your prediction is still running there, it will complete and the result URL will be visible — you can download it directly from the Replicate dashboard even if the browser tab timed out.

Cancel the prediction

In the Desi Jugad tool, click "Cancel" to send a cancel request to Replicate. This stops billing for that prediction. Note that you may still be charged for compute time already consumed before the cancellation was processed.

Try a smaller/faster model

If a prediction keeps failing, try switching to a faster model. For images, switch to FLUX Schnell. For video, switch to Wan 2.7. These have lower queue times and faster execution. Once you've confirmed your prompt works, switch back to the higher-quality model.

Rate limit errors

If you see a "429 rate limit" error, it means you're sending too many predictions per minute. Desi Jugad automatically waits 20 seconds and retries once. If it persists, wait a minute before trying again. Replicate's rate limits are generous for standard usage — this typically only happens when you're running many parallel generations.

Part 6: Frequently asked questions

Do I need to add a credit card to Replicate?

Yes. Replicate requires a payment method before you can run predictions. However, you're only charged for what you use — there's no monthly fee. Add the card, set a spending limit if you want extra safety, and you're good to go.

Can I use Replicate's free tier?

New accounts typically receive some free credits automatically. Beyond that, everything is pay-as-you-go. There's no ongoing free tier for inference — Replicate's business model is usage-based compute.

What happens if I clear my browser data?

Your API keys will be deleted from localStorage and you'll need to re-enter them. Nothing else changes — your Replicate account, credits, and past generations on Replicate's dashboard are unaffected.

Can someone else use my API key if they have access to my computer?

Yes, if someone has access to your browser (or your browser profile), they can read your localStorage and find your key. Treat your Replicate key like a password. Don't leave your browser logged in on shared computers. You can set spending limits on your Replicate key in the Replicate dashboard to cap potential misuse.

Why does Suno use a "wrapper" instead of an official API?

As of April 2026, Suno has not published an official public API. Several third-party services (sunoapi.org, kie.ai, piapi.ai) provide API access to Suno's models through their own infrastructure. These are unofficial but widely used. The API shapes are very similar across providers, which is why our code works with multiple wrappers. If Suno publishes an official API, we'll update the integration to use it directly.

I got a 401 error. What does that mean?

A 401 means your API key was rejected as invalid or unauthorized. Check that you pasted the full key without extra spaces. Make sure the key hasn't been deleted or rotated in your Replicate/Suno dashboard. If you created multiple tokens, make sure you're using the active one.

Is there a spending limit I can set on Replicate?

Yes. In your Replicate account settings, you can set a monthly spending limit. When you hit the limit, predictions will fail with a 402 error. This is a good safety net to set — even a $10/month limit is plenty for casual use and prevents any surprises.
