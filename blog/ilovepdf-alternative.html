The best free iLovePDF alternative in 2026 — no watermark, no daily limits

8 min read · Updated May 2026

You've hit the iLovePDF daily limit — again. Two tasks per day sounds reasonable until you're in the middle of actual work: merge a batch of invoices, compress a report, split a contract, and suddenly you're locked out until tomorrow. Or you download your compressed PDF and spot the iLovePDF watermark on the first page. Neither of these things should happen on a tool that calls itself free.

This article is written by the team at Desi Jugad, so we'll be transparent: we built an alternative. We'll give you a fair picture of what iLovePDF does well, where it falls short, and exactly how Desi Jugad compares — including the cases where iLovePDF is still the right choice.

Why iLovePDF users look for alternatives

iLovePDF is a genuinely well-designed product. The interface is clean, the tool coverage is solid for PDF work, and it's popular enough that many people encounter it through Google searches or colleague recommendations. But the free tier has two friction points that drive users away:

The 2-tasks-per-day cap. iLovePDF's free tier limits you to two PDF operations per day. For someone who processes PDFs casually — one compression a week, an occasional merge — this is fine. For anyone doing regular document work, it's a constant interruption. Real workflows don't pause for 24-hour resets.

Watermarks on some free outputs. Certain iLovePDF operations on the free tier add iLovePDF branding to the output file. The policy varies by tool and has changed over time, but the watermark has appeared on enough free-tier outputs that it's a recurring complaint. When you're sending a document to a client or colleague, invisible watermarks are not acceptable.

Beyond those two issues, there's the underlying privacy question: iLovePDF processes every file you touch on its servers in Spain. For most personal documents this doesn't matter. For a salary slip, a medical report, or an NDA, your document is now on someone else's infrastructure for the duration of processing.

How Desi Jugad handles PDF files — the technical picture

When you open a PDF tool on Desi Jugad and drop your file, here's what actually happens: your browser downloads the tool's WebAssembly module (a compiled version of the same open-source libraries — pdf-lib, PDF.js — that power desktop software). The PDF is loaded into your browser's memory. The transformation runs locally. The output file is generated locally. You download it directly from memory.

At no point does a byte of your file travel over the network. You can verify this: open your browser's developer tools, switch to the Network tab, and watch during a conversion. You'll see no outgoing request carrying your PDF data. This is the only category where the privacy claim is checkable, not just a matter of trusting a privacy policy.

For most PDF operations — compression, merging, splitting, rotating, protecting — this approach is as fast as or faster than server-based tools because it eliminates the upload-wait-download round trip. For large, highly compressed PDFs with complex vector graphics, server-side tools with more RAM may produce marginally better compression ratios.

When iLovePDF is still the better choice

Being honest about this matters. There are real scenarios where iLovePDF (or Smallpdf) is the right tool:

PDF annotation and highlighting. iLovePDF lets you add comments, highlights, and drawings directly onto PDF pages. This is useful for reviewing contracts, marking up reports, or collaborative document review. Desi Jugad doesn't offer in-browser annotation.

PDF form filling and e-signatures. iLovePDF supports filling in PDF form fields and adding legally valid e-signatures. For signing contracts remotely, this is a significant feature. Browser-based alternatives don't currently match this capability.

PDF to Word / OCR. Converting a scanned PDF to an editable Word document requires OCR (optical character recognition). This is computationally intensive and not yet practical in a browser. iLovePDF's server-side OCR is good for this specific use case.

Mobile-first workflows. iLovePDF's mobile app is polished and optimised for phone use. If you're regularly processing PDFs on a smartphone, the native app experience is smoother than a browser tool.

For these features, iLovePDF Pro at €6/month is reasonable. For everything else — compress, merge, split, rotate, protect, convert — Desi Jugad covers the same ground with no limits and no upload.

Frequently asked questions

Why does iLovePDF limit free users to 2 tasks per day?

iLovePDF is a freemium service — server-side PDF processing has real infrastructure costs, and the 2-task-per-day cap is designed to convert active users to paying subscribers. For light use it's workable; for regular document work it becomes a daily frustration. Browser-based tools like Desi Jugad have no per-task infrastructure cost because the processing happens on your device, so there's no economic reason to impose a daily cap.

Does iLovePDF add a watermark to free documents?

iLovePDF adds branding or watermarks on some free-tier operations — notably on some conversion and editing tools. The specific operations affected have changed over time. Desi Jugad never adds any watermark, branding, or metadata to output files. Your output is identical to what a paid tool would produce.

Does iLovePDF upload my files to a server?

Yes. iLovePDF processes files on its servers in Spain and the EU. Files travel over HTTPS and are deleted after processing. For sensitive documents — salary slips, medical records, legal contracts — browser-based processing is meaningfully safer because there is no server upload step at all. On Desi Jugad, the file exists only in your browser's memory and never touches the network.

When is iLovePDF better than Desi Jugad?

iLovePDF is ahead for PDF annotation and highlighting, e-signatures, PDF-to-Word OCR conversion, and mobile-app workflows.
