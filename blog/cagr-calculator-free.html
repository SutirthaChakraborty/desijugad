CAGR Calculator — Measure Investment Returns Accurately

Published April 27, 2026

What is CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)?

CAGR is the rate at which an investment grows per year, accounting for compounding. It smooths out year-to-year volatility to show the true average annual return. You can't use simple percentage gain because it ignores time value of money and compounding effects.

Why CAGR Matters

Imagine comparing two investments:

  • Investment A: ₹1 lakh → ₹3 lakh in 10 years (200% total gain)
  • Investment B: ₹1 lakh → ₹2 lakh in 5 years (100% gain)

Which is better? You can't tell without CAGR! A outperforms B on CAGR (12% vs 15%), but A made more total money. CAGR normalizes for time.

CAGR Formula

CAGR = (Ending Value ÷ Starting Value)^(1 ÷ Years) - 1

For the above: Investment A's CAGR = (3 lakh ÷ 1 lakh)^(1/10) - 1 = 11.6% annual

Beating Inflation with CAGR

Inflation averages 6% annually in India. To grow real wealth, your investments must beat inflation:

  • FD at 6.5% CAGR barely beats inflation (real return: 0.5%)
  • Balanced Fund at 11% CAGR beats inflation by 5% (real return: 5%)
  • Equity Fund at 13% CAGR beats inflation by 7% (real return: 7%)

Real-World CAGR Example

Nifty 50 CAGR over different periods (as of 2026):

  • Last 5 years: 13.2%
  • Last 10 years: 12.8%
  • Last 20 years: 11.4%

Even during market crashes, 20-year CAGR remains solid — proving that long-term investing smooths volatility.

How to Use CAGR for Investment Decisions

  1. Check CAGR of different funds/stocks over 3/5/10 years
  2. Compare with benchmark (Nifty 50, Sensex)
  3. Adjust for risk — high CAGR with high volatility may not be worth it
  4. Look for consistent CAGR (not just lucky years)

Conclusion

CAGR is your weapon against misleading marketing claims about investment returns. Always ask for CAGR, not total percentage gain. A calculator makes this comparison instant and accurate.
