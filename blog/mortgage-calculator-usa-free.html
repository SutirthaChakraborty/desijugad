Mortgage Calculator USA — Monthly Payment, Interest & Affordability 2026

Published April 28, 2026 · 10 min read

Buying a home is the largest financial decision most Americans make. Calculating your monthly mortgage payment before you start shopping helps you understand what you can afford and how much you'll pay in total. This guide covers the mortgage calculation formula, current rates, and affordability rules.

→ Use the free loan/EMI calculator for monthly payment estimates

How to Calculate Monthly Mortgage Payment

The monthly payment formula is: M = P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n – 1]

  • P = Principal loan amount
  • r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
  • n = Number of payments (years × 12)

Example: $400,000 Home Purchase

  • Home price: $400,000
  • Down payment: $80,000 (20%)
  • Loan amount: $320,000
  • Interest rate: 6.75% (2026 average 30-year fixed)
  • Monthly payment: $2,076
  • Total paid over 30 years: $747,360
  • Total interest: $427,360

US Mortgage Rates 2026

  • 30-year fixed: ~6.5–7.0% (varies with Fed rates)
  • 15-year fixed: ~5.8–6.3% (lower rate, higher monthly payment)
  • 5/1 ARM: ~5.5–6.0% initial (resets after 5 years)
  • FHA loan (3.5% down): ~7.0–7.5%
  • VA loan (for veterans): ~6.0–6.5%

Check bankrate.com or nerdwallet.com for current daily rates. Use our calculator with the latest rate.

The 28/36 Rule — How Much Can You Afford?

Financial advisors recommend:

  • 28% rule: Housing costs (mortgage + taxes + insurance) should not exceed 28% of gross monthly income
  • 36% rule: Total debt payments (housing + all other debt) should not exceed 36% of gross monthly income

Example: If your gross monthly income is $7,000:

  • Maximum monthly housing cost: $7,000 × 28% = $1,960
  • Maximum total debt: $7,000 × 36% = $2,520

Other Costs Beyond the Mortgage

  • Property tax: 0.5%–2.5% of home value annually (varies by state). Texas and Illinois have high rates (~2%); California, Florida have lower effective rates.
  • Homeowner's insurance: ~$1,200–$2,400/year on average
  • PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance): Required if down payment is less than 20%. Typically 0.5–1.5% of loan amount annually
  • HOA fees: Common in condos and planned communities. $100–$500/month
  • Maintenance: Budget 1–3% of home value annually for upkeep

15-Year vs 30-Year Mortgage

  • 30-year: Lower monthly payment, much more total interest paid, builds equity slowly
  • 15-year: Higher monthly payment (~40% more), lower interest rate, builds equity faster, dramatically less total interest

Example ($300,000 loan):

  • 30-year at 6.75%: $1,945/month, total interest $400,220
  • 15-year at 6.25%: $2,573/month, total interest $163,140
  • 15-year saves $237,080 in interest but costs $628 more per month

Mortgage in the USA vs Home Loan in India

  • USA: Mortgage rates 6.5–7% (2026). 20% down payment avoids PMI. 30-year terms are standard.
  • India: Home loan rates 8.5–9.3% (2026). Tenor up to 30 years. EMI calculation is identical formula.
  • Pakistan: Islamic financing (Shariah-compliant) is common. Bank Islami, Meezan Bank offer Musharaka/Diminishing Musharaka arrangements.

Frequently Asked Questions

What credit score do I need for a US mortgage?

Conventional loan: minimum 620 FICO. Best rates: 740+. FHA loan: 580 minimum (with 3.5% down), 500 minimum (with 10% down). VA loan: No official minimum but lenders typically require 580–620.

What is the difference between pre-qualification and pre-approval?

Pre-qualification is an informal estimate based on self-reported info. Pre-approval involves a formal credit check and income verification — sellers take pre-approved offers much more seriously.

Can I pay off my mortgage early?

Yes. Most US mortgages have no prepayment penalty. Extra principal payments reduce the loan balance and total interest dramatically. Even one extra payment per year can cut years off a 30-year mortgage.
